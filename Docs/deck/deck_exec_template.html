The problemWhy option surfaces are hard

Listed option markets are sparse, noisy, and structurally constrained — most of the surface is never quoted.

  • Wide, asymmetric spreads — wings quote at multiples of ATM spread; mids are not fair value.
  • American exercise — listed equity quotes embed an early-exercise premium that poisons European-model fits.
  • Structural no-arbitrage — butterfly and calendar constraints couple every strike and expiry; a fit that ignores them produces tradable-looking nonsense.
  • Events distort the clock — earnings compress into a day what diffusion spreads over weeks.
  • Most names are dark — beyond the top tickers, whole expiries have no usable quotes at all.

The raw material — live SPY chain (Massive feed). Per strike: bid / mid / ask implied volatility, the model's fitted value, and the deviation. The fitter's job is to decide which of these numbers to believe, and by how much.

Why it matters Trading desks need a system that turns partial, contradictory evidence into a full, arbitrage-clean surface with uncertainty.

The systemOne pipeline, four models, one vocabulary

One quote pipeline feeds four smile models — all speaking the desk's own "handles": ATM vol, skew, curvature, wings.

Every quote passes one deterministic preparation pipeline (forwards, de-Americanization, liquidity weighting); every model reads and writes the same handle coordinates — which is what lets priors, filters and the graph act on any model's output without translation.

  • LQD, the house model — parametrizes the return distribution itself, so the implied density is positive by construction: no butterfly arbitrage inside a slice, by design rather than by penalty.
  • SVI-JW, the market standard — fitted natively in raw SVI, with the exact jump-wing conversion domain-proved and test-locked (handle-based entry/bump/export: roadmap).
  • Multi-Core Sigmoid, the event model — fits the W-shaped smiles around earnings that convex models cannot reach, with hard guardrails (parameters capped by quote count, arbitrage measured on every fit).
  • Local Vol, the non-parametric surface — a piecewise-affine local-variance grid calibrated through the pricing PDE itself; positive nodes ⇒ positive surface ⇒ arbitrage-free prices.

The Parametric workspace — live SPY (Massive feed). Blue — the fitted smile; red marks — quoted bid/ask per strike. The aside reports the handles directly: ATM vol, skew, curvature, wing scales, Lee slopes, var-swap level — model parameters, not chart read-offs.

Why it matters Handles are the desk vocabulary — every model, prior, filter and graph update speaks the same coordinates, so nothing is a black box in translation.

Quote preparationMaking US-listed quotes fittable

Before any model sees a quote: early exercise removed, forwards stabilized, spreads respected.

  • De-Americanization — US-listed quotes embed an early-exercise premium; each is inverted through a binomial tree to its European-equivalent vol. On real SPY data (chart) the implied-vol effect on in-the-money puts runs a median +519 bp — a stress exhibit on the side production discards; the fitted OTM population sees a median ~4 bp, tens in the put wing. Compiled kernel: ~60× on wide chains.
  • Forwards implied from the chain itself — put–call parity as a regression; the poorly-identified discount is clamped to a physical band. A production case file: a delayed feed's stale pairs tore the ATM smile — the clamp healed it, byte-identical on clean chains.
  • Fit to the band, not the mid — a mid inside a wide wing spread is not fair value, so staying anywhere inside the quoted spread is free and only leaving it costs. Noisy wing prints stop dragging kinks into the smile; three targets (mid / band / haircut) span the styles desks actually use.

Real market data, not a toy. One live SPY expiry (Massive capture, ~6 months): every put mid inverted two ways — as if European (red) vs de-Americanized (teal). Out of the money they agree to a few bp; in the money the model-implied IV effect of the premium runs a median +519 bp, up to +814 bp — a stress exhibit on the side production discards; the fitted OTM population sees a median ~4 bp, tens in the put wing.

Why it matters Single names and dividend payers become fittable, every IV stands on a defensible forward, and the fitter only pays to leave the quoted spread — exactly how a trader thinks about it.

No-arbitrageDiscipline, with case files

Arbitrage-free means measured and enforced where data lives — never invented where it doesn't.

  • Cleanliness is a number, not a claim — every fit reports its butterfly diagnostic (the Durrleman g(k), proportional to the implied probability density); calendar consistency is checked in total variance across expiries.
  • Penalties are exactly zero on clean surfaces — enforcement is byte-identical to OFF when the market is admissible: a clean fit is never distorted.
  • Production case files became design rules — a calendar check sampled in unquoted strikes manufactured 151 bp of phantom penalty and flattened far-dated fits (fix: constraints confined to the traded range); the event model once invented a put wing on illiquid chains (fix: a measured, ablation-tested fence). Both are documented and test-locked.
  • Wings obey moment logic — every model's tail extrapolation is a stated contract under Lee's universal slope cap; deep OTM marks inherit discipline from theory, not from the last two quotes.

Regenerated on every build. Left — all implied densities non-negative: no butterfly arbitrage, by construction. Right — stacked total variance, non-crossing at every strike: the actual calendar-arbitrage check, in the same coordinates as the app's Stacked IV tab (measured on independently fitted slices; enforcement is Note 10) — produced end-to-end by the production calibrator.

Why it matters A surface you can quote from: no-arbitrage enforcement that repairs violations where quoted and never distorts a clean market.

DynamicsSpot moves · memory · noise vs news

The surface moves with spot by rule, remembers yesterday only where today is silent — and tells noise from news.

  • Spot–vol transport (SSR) — when spot moves, every mark moves instantly by the desk's chosen stickiness rule (sticky-strike, sticky-moneyness, sticky-local-vol — one dial spans them all); recalibration then absorbs only genuine news.
  • Prior persistence — the fit remembers yesterday's smile shape, not its level, and each pull is gated to exactly zero wherever today's quotes speak. Validated on history: ~32 bp better held-out wing reconstruction, 66% win rate over 1,116 nodes (Aug-2024 spike regime; cross-regime rerun queued) — and two candidate designs that never beat "no prior" are reported, not hidden.
  • The observation filter — a Kalman filter on the handles separates quote noise from genuine surface motion, believing today in proportion to its measured precision. Tuned by 39,190 backtested steps; on days when quotes contradict the chain it beats the raw fit outright (5.6 vs 9.8 bp) with honest uncertainty bands; on outright shock days the raw fit still wins — the flagged fix before active-by-default.

GraphThe differentiator, validated

The propagation layer carries news, never levels, to unquoted smiles — and is validated by leave-one-out on history.

Nodes are (underlying, expiry) smiles; edges couple adjacent expiries and related names. Only calibration innovations propagate, weighted by each lit fit's own precision — a dark node keeps its own personality and inherits its neighbours' surprises, with uncertainty attached. Leave-one-out (LOO): pretend a lit node is dark, reconstruct it from the others, compare to the calibration it actually had.

+8…+14 bp
fully-dark single names behind lit indexes/ETFs — Aug-2024 spike, the product case
+3.8…+7.2 bp
same, fully out-of-sample (Oct-2022 bear) — 25 assets × 3 regimes, ~47k held-out scores
  • Decisive skill where the graph has support — neighbour-supported indexes +10…+76 bp, ETFs +3…+7 bp across all three regimes; unbiased bands (ζ ≈ 0, std 0.70–1.10 in stress).
  • Honest scope — in calm, earnings-idiosyncratic tape the dark-name skill is ≈0 (never negative): there is no systematic signal to carry, and that regime's bands read overconfident (flagged, fix queued).
  • Every edge is a number a trader can defend — weights and per-edge β ("NVDA moves 1.3× QQQ") live in a persisted editor, and the same LOO backtest re-certifies the graph on the desk's own universe, one click.

Leave-one-out, plotted. Held-out node predictions versus realized calibrations: the graph posterior tightens errors over the transported-prior baseline, and its standardized residuals sit where a correct model's should.

Why it matters The graph formalizes what traders do informally — "SPY repriced, so my dark midcap should too, a little" — with a posterior and a band instead of a hunch.
