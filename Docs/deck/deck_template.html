The problemWhy option surfaces are hard

Listed option markets are sparse, noisy, and structurally constrained — most of the surface is never quoted.

  • Wide, asymmetric spreads — wings quote at multiples of ATM spread; mids are not fair value.
  • American exercise — listed equity quotes embed an early-exercise premium that poisons European-model fits.
  • Structural no-arbitrage — butterfly and calendar constraints couple every strike and expiry; a fit that ignores them produces tradable-looking nonsense.
  • Events distort the clock — earnings compress into a day what diffusion spreads over weeks.
  • Most names are dark — beyond the top tickers, whole expiries have no usable quotes at all.

The raw material — live SPY chain (Massive feed). Per strike: bid / mid / ask implied volatility (IV), the model's fitted IV, and the deviation. The fitter's job is to decide which of these numbers to believe, and by how much.

Trading relevance Trading desks need a system that turns partial, contradictory evidence into a full, arbitrage-clean surface with uncertainty.

VocabularyEight words the deck uses precisely

Eight terms, defined once — everything after this slide uses them exactly.

Handle
A smile descriptor a desk quotes in: ATM vol, skew, curvature, wing slopes. In this app they are model parameters (or exact functions of them), not chart read-offs.
Node · lit / dark
One (underlying, expiry) smile — the unit of everything here. Lit = has usable quotes, gets calibrated; dark = unquoted, gets reconstructed.
Transported prior
Yesterday's fitted surface, moved to today's spot by the chosen spot-vol rule (SSR) — the "no news" baseline for every node.
Innovation
Today's calibration minus the transported prior, per handle — the genuine news in a lit node. The only thing the graph propagates.
Posterior · credible band
The estimate after combining sources (prior + news), with its uncertainty. A dark node's posterior comes with a band it can be judged against later.
RMS · bp
Fit error = root-mean-square implied-vol error, in vol basis points (1 bp = 0.01 vol point). 10 bp ≈ tighter than most quoted spreads.
ζ (standardized residual)
Error divided by its own predicted uncertainty — computed wherever the system quotes a band: graph reconstructions, the LOO backtest, the observation filter. If the bands are honest, ζ ≈ standard normal: mean 0 (unbiased), std 1 (calibrated).
Static no-arbitrage
What "arbitrage-free" means here: butterfly-clean within each slice, calendar-clean across expiries. Not a claim about dynamic or model risk.

Models · LQDThe house smile model

LQD, the house model: the density is positive by construction, and the ATM handles are exact.

LQD = Log-Quantile-Density. Instead of parametrizing the implied-vol curve and hoping the implied density stays positive, the model parametrizes the log of the quantile density of the terminal return — an object whose exponential is positive by definition. Positive density ⇔ no butterfly arbitrage, so admissibility is built in, not enforced.

Log-quantile-density basis — positive density by construction
{{EQ:lqd_basis}}
ℓ = log q — log quantile density of the log-return (q = Q′, Q the quantile function); u — probability level in (0,1); L, R — left/right endpoint (tail-scale) coefficients; an — body coefficients; Pn — Legendre polynomial of degree n in 1−2u; N — body order.
Exact ATM handles — vol, skew, curvature in closed form
{{EQ:lqd_handles}}
σ₀ — ATM implied vol; s₀ — ATM skew dσ/dk; κ₀ — ATM curvature d²σ/dk²; w₀ — ATM total variance (solves Black(0,w₀) = C(0)); w₁, w₂ — 1st/2nd log-strike derivatives of w(k) at k = 0, exact by implicit differentiation; T — expiry.
  • Butterfly-free by construction — the density is an exponential; no penalty needed within a slice.
  • One hard constraint only: AR < 1 — the right tail scale AR = exp(R + Σn(−1)nan) — exactly a finite forward; Lee slopes come out structural, not tuned.
  • Analytic Jacobian via implicit-root cancellation — two model evaluations per optimizer step regardless of degree: a 12-parameter LQD fits in SVI's ~30 ms league.

Models · LQDThe fitted distribution, read directly

An LQD fit is a probability law — its density and quantile views read straight off the model.

Any fitted smile implies a risk-neutral distribution of the terminal return. LQD (Log-Quantile-Density, the house model of the previous slides) parametrizes that distribution directly — its parameter is the log of the quantile density ℓ(u) — so the density and quantile views below are the model read in its native coordinates, not a numerical second derivative of a fitted IV curve.

Generated by the production LQD fitter. Left — the risk-neutral density behind a fitted LQD smile, non-negative everywhere by construction. Right — the log-quantile-density ℓ(u) itself, plotted against the quantile level u: the model's native coordinate, where each wing is one endpoint scale (AL, AR) and the body is a smooth Legendre expansion.

Pricing identity in logit coordinates — no Black inversion in the loop
{{EQ:lqd_pricing}}
C(k) — normalized call at log-moneyness k; zk — logit point solving Q(zk) = k (Q = log-return quantile); uk = Λ(zk) — probability level (Λ = logistic function); A(z) — upper asset-share integral ∫z∞ eQ(s)Λ(s)(1−Λ(s))ds.
Structural tails — Lee slopes from the endpoint scales
{{EQ:lqd_tail}}
βL, βR — left/right asymptotic slopes of total variance w(k)/|k|; ψ — Lee's moment function ψ(p) = 2−4(√(p²+p)−p); p — critical moment exponent of ST/FT; AL, AR — LQD endpoint scales (quantile tail slopes; AR < 1 ⇔ finite forward).
  • LQD prices come from a closed-form asset-share integral — quotes, densities, quantiles and var-swaps all read off the same fitted object.
  • Tail behaviour is diagnosed, not assumed: the endpoint scales AL, AR and the implied Lee slopes are reported with every fit.
Trading relevance Because the density is the model, digital prices, tail odds and var-swap strikes are consistent with the smile by construction — not by post-processing.

Models · SVI-JWThe industry benchmark, done carefully

SVI, the market's standard smile — spoken here in jump-wing handles, with the conversion domain made explicit.

SVI ("Stochastic Volatility Inspired", Gatheral) is the market-standard 5-parameter smile; most desks and vendors quote surfaces in it. The jump-wings (JW) reparametrization re-expresses the same curve in trader units: level, skew, two wing slopes, minimum.

Raw SVI — what the optimizer sees
{{EQ:svi_raw}}
w(k) — total implied variance σ²BST; k — log-moneyness ln(K/F); a — level; b — overall wing steepness; ρ ∈ (−1,1) — tilt (wing asymmetry); m — vertex shift in k; σ — vertex rounding width.
Jump-wing handles — what humans see (exact, lossless on its domain)
{{EQ:svi_jw}}
v — ATM variance w(0)/t; ψ — ATM skew; p, c — put/call wing slopes b(1−ρ)/√w₀, b(1+ρ)/√w₀; ṽ — minimum variance; t — expiry; w₀ = vt — ATM total variance; a, b, ρ, m, σ — the raw SVI parameters above.
  • Feasible by reparametrization — softplus/tanh/exp transforms keep every optimizer iterate a valid slice.
  • Analytic Levenberg–Marquardt Jacobian — shipped after the backtest showed the default finite-difference gradients were the cost driver: 26.3 → 10.2 ms per fit (~2.6×), identical solution.
  • The raw↔JW conversion is exact on a documented domain — including the ψ=0 coordinate singularity, stated as a contract instead of hidden.

The five jump-wing handles on one slice. The level v at the money, the skew ψ drawn as the actual tangent at k = 0, the two asymptotic wing slopes p and c, and the smile minimum ṽ. The desk reads and bumps these; the optimizer never leaves raw coordinates.

Trading relevance Full interoperability with the market's standard model — SVI surfaces can be imported, fitted, bumped and exported in the units every counterparty uses.

Models · SVI-JWArbitrage fencing

Keeping SVI arbitrage-free: soft penalties that are exactly zero on admissible slices.

Two classical conditions keep a single smile arbitrage-free: the Durrleman function g(k) ≥ 0 (equivalent to a non-negative implied density — no butterfly arbitrage) and Lee's bound capping how fast the wings may grow. SVI does not satisfy them automatically, so the calibrator fences it. Scope, stated precisely: static cleanliness — butterfly here, calendar on a later slide — not dynamic completeness.

Durrleman butterfly function + Lee cap in JW units
{{EQ:durrleman}}
g(k) — Durrleman function (g ≥ 0 everywhere ⇔ non-negative density, no butterfly); w, w′, w″ — total variance and its k-derivatives; k — log-moneyness; p, c — JW put/call wing slopes; v — ATM variance; t — expiry; 2 — Lee's cap on the asymptotic variance slope.
  • g(k) ≥ 0 is the butterfly test — reported as a per-fit diagnostic for every model, not just penalized.
  • Penalties are inactive on clean slices — a fit that satisfies no-arbitrage pays nothing; enforcement is byte-identical to OFF.
  • Even the measurement was audited: an earlier arbitrage metric took finite differences of reconstructed prices, and its numerical noise flagged 28.3% of provably-clean LQD slices. The shipped metric evaluates g(k) analytically on the model's own density — the true rate reads 0.0%.

Models · MCSEvent-shaped smiles

Multi-Core Sigmoid, the event-smile model: fitting the W shapes a convex model cannot reach.

Around a scheduled binary event (earnings, CPI, FDA…) the market prices a mixture of outcomes, and the smile develops shoulders — a "W". MCS = Multi-Core Sigmoid, a kernel-based implied-variance model: a six-parameter sigmoid base that owns the wings, plus up to R localized "hat" kernels (the cores) that add the shoulders without being able to touch the tails.

The six-parameter sigmoid base
{{EQ:siv_base}}
V₀ — level; S₀ — slope; K₀ ≥ 0 — convexity; z₀ — centre; κP, κC — put/call wing steepness; z = k/(σref√t) — log-moneyness in stdevs; Φκ — log-cosh primitive.
The full model — base + R signed zero-wing hats
{{EQ:siv_model}}
vR — full MCS variance slice; vbase — the base above; R — number of hats (cores); αr — signed hat amplitude; B — zero-wing hat kernel centred cr, half-width hr, steepness κr.
The hat kernel — a normalized second difference of the same log-cosh primitive
{{EQ:siv_hat}}
B — hat kernel: 1 at its centre c, vanishing (with its derivatives) in both wings; h — half-width; κ — steepness; u = z − c.
  • Hats never move the wings (zero-wing lemma): B decays to zero on both sides, so humps and notches leave the asymptotic slopes untouched.

A W-smile, fitted and decomposed. Left — the MCS fit through an event-shaped slice a convex model cannot reach. Right — the decomposition: smooth sigmoid base plus localized hat kernels, each with its own centre, width and sign.

Trading relevance Earnings weeks stop being "the expiry we can't fit" — the event structure is captured without spline chaos and without contaminating the wings.

Models · MCSExpressiveness needs guardrails

MCS guardrails: a model powerful enough to fit a W is powerful enough to invent one.

Where quotes are dense, the data pins MCS's hats. On a thin chain, the optimizer can park a hat where quotes are stale or missing and "improve" the fit by bending the smile into negative implied probabilities — butterfly arbitrage nobody would trade. A census of real illiquid chains found 64% of these violations in the put wing. Three guardrails answer it.

  • 1 · A hard budget. A hat costs 4 parameters on top of the 6 base ones, so hats are admitted only while parameters stay below the quote count — R ≤ (Nquotes−6)/4 — and production caps R at 2 outright: the backtest showed a third hat buys no out-of-sample accuracy.
  • 2 · A fence where the data ends. The same Durrleman certificate g(k) used to fence SVI is sampled on a grid reaching past the quoted strikes, and the fit is charged only where g < 0 — twice as hard on the put side, where the census put the violations. The hinge is exactly zero on a clean fit: liquid names are byte-identical with the fence on or off.
3 · Proof by ablation — one real node, all four cells

Models · Local VolThe non-parametric pricing surface

Local Vol, the non-parametric model: a piecewise-affine local-variance surface calibrated through the pricing PDE itself.

Local volatility is the state-dependent instantaneous volatility σ(t, S) consistent with today's option prices (Dupire). Here it is not extracted from a fitted smile by differentiation; it is calibrated directly from observed option prices, without modeling the IV curve at all: the unknowns are local-variance values on a maturity × strike grid, priced through the forward pricing equation (a partial differential equation, PDE) in the spirit of Andreasen–Huge.

Forward Dupire equation — the implicit pricing map
{{EQ:dupire}}
c(T,x) — forward-normalized call price; T — maturity (event-weighted variance time); x = K/FT — normalized strike; ν(T,x) = σ²loc — the local variance being calibrated; (1−x)⁺ — payoff initial condition.
  • Piecewise affine, concretely: each rectangle of the maturity × strike grid is split into two triangles; ν(t,x) is exactly affine on each triangle (a bilinear patch would not be) and continuous across edges. The unknowns are the nodal local variances, and every surface value is a convex mix of its triangle's three corners: positive nodes ⇒ positive surface ⇒ arbitrage-free prices.
  • Quotes are fitted through the forward PDE (Andreasen–Huge philosophy): the surface that prices the market is found directly, not bootstrapped slice by slice.
  • Wings: beyond the last strike node the local variance extrapolates flat (controlled-linear on the put side when a var-swap pins the deep tail); an opt-in convexity hinge acts only in the extrapolation tail (≤ 5Δ put, below the deepest quote) — never on quoted strikes.
  • Lee's bound needs no penalty here — bounded positive local variance with flat/linear wings keeps the implied wing slopes below Lee's cap of 2, measured and reported, not assumed.

Local Vol workspace — live SPY (Massive feed). The calibrated nodal local-vol heatmap over maturity × strike, with the arbitrage badge, per-expiry error table and var-swap panel. Smile, densities, term structure and implied-vol surface views are all reconstructed from this one calibrated object.

Models · Local VolEngineering for real calibration loops

The Local Vol calibrator is engineered like a production solver, not a research script.

Calibrating local vol solves the pricing PDE hundreds of times inside an optimizer, so three numerics must be beyond suspicion: a time-stepping scheme that cannot oscillate, exact gradients, and an inner solve fast enough to live in the loop. Each is addressed explicitly.

Implicit Euler step — unconditionally stable and monotone
{{EQ:lv_step}}
Un — grid call prices at time level n; B = I − ΔT·A — implicit-step matrix (an M-matrix: B⁻¹ ≥ 0 ⇒ ordered, positive prices); ΔT — time step; A — tridiagonal discretization of ½νx²∂xx (ν local variance, x normalized strike).
  • Why implicit Euler and not Crank–Nicolson? Crank–Nicolson, the textbook scheme, is more accurate per step but only monotone when the step is small relative to variance — beyond that, the payoff kink excites its classic oscillations, which read as negative densities. The implicit step is monotone at any step size (B⁻¹ ≥ 0 above: prices stay ordered and positive), trading one order of time accuracy for that unconditional guarantee.
  • Exact gradients of the scheme actually solved — the PDE march itself is differentiated (every sensitivity reuses the price solve's tridiagonal factorization), so the optimizer never sees a finite-difference approximation: fits are ~1.3–1.65× faster and exactly reproducible.
  • Compiled inner loop (Numba): 6.5× the standard LAPACK banded solve, identical to ~1e-15, with a plain-NumPy fallback when Numba is absent.

Vol-Fitter · capabilities — Technical Note 04 · Local volatility
Quote preparationDe-Americanization

American quotes are converted to European-equivalent vols before any model sees them.

US-listed single-stock and ETF options are American: they can be exercised early, so their quotes embed an early-exercise premium a European model does not know about. A CRR binomial tree values that right (the exercise decision rolls backward through a discrete spot lattice), and each quote is inverted through it: find the vol at which the American tree price matches the quote — the premium is removed, strike by strike.

Per-strike CRR inversion — return the European-equivalent Black vol
{{EQ:deam}}
A — observed American option price; CRRAm(σ) — American value from the Cox–Ross–Rubinstein binomial tree at vol σ (given spot, strike, expiry, rate, dividends); σ* — the root, returned as the European-equivalent Black vol.
  • The tree only prices the American/European difference — its discretization error largely cancels in that difference (the control-variate role). Discrete cash dividends use an escrowed-spot lattice: the tree diffuses the spot net of the present value of future dividends (cash drops would otherwise stop it recombining) and adds them back at each node, so exercise is checked against the true cum-dividend spot.
  • Compiled CRR kernel (Numba): ~60× on wide chains — cached under a digest of the only inputs de-Am depends on (chain, forward, discount, dividends, clock): re-tuning a fit reuses the conversion; trees re-run only on a data change.
  • Caution, learned in production: a whole-curve convexity repair gapped live SPY/NVDA smiles — its free global baseline let a wing repair re-tilt the whole curve, moving ATM prices a sub-penny: negligible in price, large in ATM vol, where vega peaks. Reverted; the shipped repair is wing-only, band-constrained, ATM core exactly fixed.

Real market data, not a toy. One live SPY expiry (Massive capture, Jun 2026, ~6 months): every put mid inverted two ways — as if European (red) vs de-Americanized through the tree (teal). Out of the money the two agree to a few bp; in the money the wedge is the early-exercise premium — median +519 bp, up to +817 bp — which would poison any European fit fed the raw quotes.

Trading relevance Single names and dividend payers become fittable: early exercise is removed before modeling, and the repair never invents information where the market quoted none.
Vol-Fitter · capabilities — Technical Note 05 · De-Americanization
Quote preparationForwards & dividends · case file inside

Forwards are implied from the chain itself — and the worst-identified parameter is clamped, not trusted.

Every implied vol depends on the forward F and the discount factor D used to compute it. Both are estimated from the option chain itself — put–call parity holds model-free, so a regression of C−P on the strike recovers them with no external curve. The catch: the regression slope (the discount) is poorly identified on noisy chains, and an error there drags the forward and tears the ATM smile.

Put–call parity as a regression — slope is the discount, level is the forward
{{EQ:parity}}
C(K), P(K) — call/put mid prices at strike K; D = e−rT — discount factor (= minus the regression slope); F — forward (= intercept / discount); a, b — intercept and slope of the least-squares line C−P = a + bK.
Case file — the delayed feed that gapped the ATM smile

A delayed data tier quoted stale pairs; the parity slope drifted to discounts above 1 (negative rates), dragging the forward and tearing a gap into the ATM smile. Fix: clamp the implied rate to a physical band and re-derive the forward from the well-identified price level. Clean chains are byte-identical; the broken feed heals.

  • Four dividend modes (yield, discrete cash, blends) + a de-bias loop that holds the discount fixed while removing American bias from the parity inputs.

Forwards workspace — live SPY (Massive feed). The forward curve with per-expiry parity-implied vs theoretical carry values, the source of each, manual overrides, and a click-to-edit discrete dividend schedule that feeds both the forward model and the de-Americanization lattice.

Trading relevance Every IV in the system stands on a defensible forward — estimated from the market, stabilized against feed pathologies, and overridable when the desk knows better.
Vol-Fitter · capabilities — Technical Note 06 · Forwards & dividends
CalibrationOne objective, every model

The calibration objective: vol error, inside the quoted spread — with three fit targets (mid, band, haircut).

A mid quote is not fair value — it is the midpoint of a spread that can be half a vol point wide in the wings. So all models share one objective: residuals normalized by vega (the loss reads in vol units), bid–ask bands (only leaving the quoted band costs anything), and liquidity-aware weights.

CalibrationWing & tail discipline

Wing extrapolation obeys moment logic — Lee's bound caps every model's tails, each by its own mechanism.

Lee's moment formula: total implied variance can grow at most linearly in |log-moneyness|, with slope at most 2, and the slope is tied to how many moments the terminal distribution has. A steeper wing implies an infinite price for some traded payoff — free money. It is the one universal law of smile extrapolation.

DynamicsSpot–vol transport (SSR)

When spot moves, the surface moves by rule, before recalibration — one transport realizes any skew-stickiness ratio.

SSR = skew-stickiness ratio: how much ATM vol moves when spot moves, in units of the skew. The classic regimes are points on one dial — R = 0 "sticky-moneyness" (the smile rides with spot), R = 1 "sticky-strike" (vol per strike frozen), R = 2 "sticky-local-vol". One analytic transport realizes any value in between, instantly and without refitting.

The SSR transport — one formula spans sticky-strike to sticky-moneyness and beyond
{{EQ:ssr_transport}}
σnew, σold — post-/pre-move smiles in log-moneyness k (vs the prevailing forward); δ = ln(Fnew/Fold) — log-forward move; SSR — skew-stickiness ratio (0 sticky-moneyness, 1 sticky-strike, ≈2 sticky-local-vol); s₀ — ATM skew ∂σ/∂k at k = 0.
  • SSR is the dial: R = 0 sticky-moneyness, R = 1 sticky-strike, R = 2 sticky-local-vol — and anything between, from one analytic map.
  • Sticky-local-vol, exactly: freeze the local-vol surface in absolute strike and move the forward — the new smile is then fully determined: the old curve read at Hagan's relabeled log-moneyness ℓ(k,δ), which ≈ k + 2δ for small moves — precisely where SSR ≈ 2 comes from.
  • The calibrated LV grid transports by one bookkeeping rule: its strike nodes follow the fraction 1 − R/2 of the spot move (all of it at R = 0, half at R = 1, frozen in strike at R = 2) — pure node relabeling, no refit; only the fully exact sticky-LV reprice costs one Dupire PDE solve.
  • The classic sign trap is documented, not discovered: a transported curve reads the old smile at k + δ, while a fixed-strike quote's moneyness label moves to k − δ. Both signs are correct and both are in production; mixing them flips the skew response — so they live in a stated assumption block, test-locked.

DynamicsPrior persistence — trust-gated memory

Prior persistence: the fit remembers yesterday's smile shape, not its level — and only where today's quotes are silent.

The idea in one line: carry yesterday's surface into today's fit, but only where today's market says nothing. Mechanically, the prior adds a few soft pull-terms to the fit — "stay near yesterday's spot-transported value" — one per smile feature (ATM level, skew, curvature, wings). Each pull's weight is gated: exactly zero for any feature today's quotes already pin down.

One universal residual shape — model vs transported prior, per feature
{{EQ:prior_residual}}
rj — penalty row for feature j; Oj — feature functional (delta-located call price, ATM/RR/BF/var-swap basket, or smile factor), applied to today's model and to the transported prior; λj — gated weight; scalej — the feature's natural scale.
The activation gate — exactly zero where data is sufficient
{{EQ:prior_gate}}
gap — prior activation in [0,1] (1 = data silent, exactly 0 once identified); πobs — observed precision of the feature (its quote support today); πreq — required precision threshold; γ — sharpness of the on/off edge.
  • No-damp guarantee — a feature today's quotes identify precisely is never pulled toward yesterday (proved, and test-locked).
  • Skew and curvature are remembered as baskets — risk-reversal / butterfly combinations that are unchanged when the whole smile shifts level — so after an overnight jump the fit keeps yesterday's shape without fighting the new level. "Shape, not level" is a property of the construction, not a slogan.

The gate, read left to right. x-axis — how precisely today's quotes pin the feature, as a fraction of the required precision; y-axis — how strongly the prior pulls (1 = full, 0 = off). DynamicsThe observation filter — noise vs news

The observation filter — a Kalman filter in handle space — separates quote noise from genuine surface motion.

Two consecutive calibrations never agree — some of the difference is the market moving, most is quote noise. The filter tells them apart the way navigation systems do: each calibration is a noisy measurement of the true handles (ATM vol, skew, curvature); yesterday's filtered state, moved by the SSR transport, is the prediction; the two are blended by relative precision. The blend weights are the Kalman gains K ∈ [0,1]: K = 1 "believe today fully", K = 0 "today is noise". (The prior of the previous slides fills gaps within a fit; the filter works across time.)

{{EQ:kalman_map}}
