The problemWhy option surfaces are hard

Listed option markets are sparse, noisy, and structurally constrained — most of the surface is never quoted.

  • Wide, asymmetric spreads — wings quote at multiples of ATM spread; mids are not fair value.
  • American exercise — listed equity quotes embed an early-exercise premium that poisons European-model fits.
  • Structural no-arbitrage — butterfly and calendar constraints couple every strike and expiry; a fit that ignores them produces tradable-looking nonsense.
  • Events distort the clock — earnings compress into a day what diffusion spreads over weeks.
  • Most names are dark — beyond the top tickers, whole expiries have no usable quotes at all.

VocabularyEight words the deck uses precisely

Eight terms, defined once — everything after this slide uses them exactly.

Handle
A smile descriptor a desk quotes in: ATM vol, skew, curvature, wing slopes. In this app they are model parameters (or exact functions of them), not chart read-offs.
Node · lit / dark
One (underlying, expiry) smile — the unit of everything here. Lit = has usable quotes, gets calibrated; dark = unquoted, gets reconstructed.
Transported prior
Yesterday's fitted surface, moved to today's spot by the chosen spot-vol rule (SSR) — the "no news" baseline for every node.
Innovation
Today's calibration minus the transported prior, per handle — the genuine news in a lit node. The only thing the graph propagates.
Posterior · credible band
The estimate after combining sources (prior + news), with its uncertainty. A dark node's posterior comes with a band it can be judged against later.
RMS · bp
Fit error = root-mean-square implied-vol error, in vol basis points (1 bp = 0.01 vol point). 10 bp ≈ tighter than most quoted spreads.
ζ (standardized residual)
Error divided by its own predicted uncertainty. If the bands are honest, ζ ≈ standard normal: mean 0 (unbiased), std 1 (calibrated).
Static no-arbitrage
What "arbitrage-free" means here: butterfly-clean within each slice, calendar-clean across expiries. Not a claim about dynamic or model risk.

Note 01 · LQDModeling backbone

The house model makes density positivity native — and exposes exact ATM handles.

LQD = Log-Quantile-Density. Instead of parametrizing the implied-vol curve and hoping the implied density stays positive, the model parametrizes the log of the quantile density of the terminal return — an object whose exponential is positive by definition. Positive density ⇔ no butterfly arbitrage, so admissibility is built in, not enforced.

Log-quantile-density basis — positive density by construction
{{EQ:lqd_basis}}
Exact ATM handles — vol, skew, curvature in closed form
{{EQ:lqd_handles}}
  • Butterfly-free by construction — the density is an exponential; no penalty needed within a slice.
  • One hard constraint only: AR < 1 (finite forward); Lee tail slopes come out structural, not tuned.
  • Analytic Jacobian via implicit-root cancellation — each optimizer step costs two model evaluations regardless of degree, so a 12-parameter LQD calibrates in the same ~30 ms league as the 5-parameter SVI.

Note 02 · SVI / SVI-JWThe industry baseline, done carefully

SVI is the lingua franca — the app speaks it in jump-wing handles, with the conversion domain made explicit.

SVI ("Stochastic Volatility Inspired", Gatheral) is the market-standard 5-parameter smile; most desks and vendors quote surfaces in it. The jump-wings (JW) reparametrization re-expresses the same curve in trader units: level, skew, two wing slopes, minimum.

Raw SVI — what the optimizer sees
{{EQ:svi_raw}}
Jump-wing handles — what humans see (exact, lossless on its domain)
{{EQ:svi_jw}}
  • Feasible by reparametrization — softplus/tanh/exp transforms keep every optimizer iterate a valid slice.

  • The raw↔JW conversion is exact on a documented domain — including the ψ=0 coordinate singularity, stated as a contract instead of hidden.

Note 02 · SVI / SVI-JWArbitrage fencing

Soft penalties that are exactly zero on admissible slices turn parametric fits into tradable surfaces.

Two classical conditions keep a single smile arbitrage-free: the Durrleman function g(k) ≥ 0 (equivalent to a non-negative implied density — no butterfly arbitrage) and Lee's bound capping how fast the wings may grow. SVI does not satisfy them automatically, so the calibrator fences it. Scope, stated precisely: static cleanliness — butterfly here, calendar on a later slide — not dynamic completeness.

Durrleman butterfly function + Lee cap in JW units
{{EQ:durrleman}}
  • g(k) ≥ 0 is the butterfly test — reported as a per-fit diagnostic for every model, not just penalized.
  • Penalties are inactive on clean slices — a fit that satisfies no-arbitrage pays nothing; enforcement is byte-identical to OFF.
  • Even the measurement was audited: an earlier arbitrage metric took finite differences of reconstructed prices, and its numerical noise flagged 28.3% of provably-clean LQD slices. The shipped metric evaluates g(k) analytically on the model's own density — the true rate reads 0.0%.

Note 03 · Multi-Core SIVEvent-shaped smiles

Event smiles make a W — a convex hyperbola cannot; zero-wing hats can.

Around a scheduled binary event (earnings, CPI, FDA…) the market prices a mixture of outcomes, and the smile develops shoulders — a "W". SIV = Sigmoid Implied Variance: a six-parameter convex base that owns the wings, plus up to R localized "hat" bumps that add the shoulders without being able to touch the tails.

The six-parameter base — level V₀, slope S₀, curvature K₀, centre z₀, one wing-curvature per side
{{EQ:siv_base}}
The full model — base + R signed zero-wing hats
{{EQ:siv_model}}
The hat kernel — a normalized second difference of the same log-cosh primitive
{{EQ:siv_hat}}
  • Hats never move the wings (zero-wing lemma): B decays to zero on both sides, so humps and notches leave the asymptotic slopes untouched.

A W-smile, fitted and decomposed. Left — the fit through an event-shaped slice a convex model cannot reach. Right — the decomposition: smooth sigmoid base plus localized hat kernels, each with its own centre, width and sign.

Trading relevance Earnings weeks stop being "the expiry we can't fit" — the event structure is captured without spline chaos and without contaminating the wings.

A model powerful enough to fit a W is powerful enough to invent one — so it ships with guardrails.

Flexibility is a liability on thin markets: given freedom, SIV's hats will happily "explain" stale wing quotes by manufacturing structure no one would trade. The guardrails below were not designed on a whiteboard — they came out of the backtest, and were ablated (each fix tested alone and together) on real chains.

Note 04 · Local volatilityThe pricing surface

A piecewise-affine local-vol surface calibrated through the PDE itself — positivity of nodes is cleanliness of the surface.

Local volatility is the state-dependent instantaneous volatility σ(t, S) consistent with today's option prices (Dupire) — the workhorse for pricing exotics and for hedging dynamics. Here it is not extracted from a fitted smile by differentiation; it is calibrated directly: local-variance values on a maturity × strike grid, priced through the forward pricing equation (a partial differential equation, PDE) in the spirit of Andreasen–Huge.

Forward Dupire equation — the implicit pricing map
{{EQ:dupire}}
  • Quotes are fitted through the forward PDE (Andreasen–Huge philosophy): the surface that prices the market is found directly, not bootstrapped slice by slice.
  • Nodal positivity ⇒ arbitrage-free surface — one theorem replaces a penalty zoo: any positive local-variance grid propagates clean prices everywhere.
  • Continuous piecewise-affine ν(t,x): interpretable nodes on a strike × maturity grid — a trader can read (and cap) the local vol directly.

Local Vol workspace — live SPY (Yahoo feed). The calibrated nodal local-vol heatmap over maturity × strike, with the arbitrage badge, per-expiry error table and var-swap panel. Smile, densities, term structure and implied-vol surface views are all reconstructed from this one calibrated object.

Note 04 · Local volatilityEngineering for real calibration loops

The local-vol calibrator is engineered like a production solver, not a research script.

A local-vol calibrator lives or dies on numerics: the stability of the time-stepping scheme, the exactness of the gradients fed to the optimizer, and the raw speed of the inner PDE solve that runs hundreds of times per calibration.

Implicit Euler step — an M-matrix, hence unconditionally stable and monotone
{{EQ:lv_step}}
  • Discrete maximum principle — the step matrix is an M-matrix (positive diagonal, non-positive off-diagonals), so there is no time-step restriction and no spurious oscillation; the popular Crank–Nicolson scheme measurably loses this.
  • Exact discrete sensitivities — the tangent and adjoint of the actual scheme (not of the continuous equation) feed a matrix-free Gauss–Newton solver, ~1.3–1.65× faster than the default trust-region method.
  • Compiled PDE march (Numba): 6.5× the LAPACK banded solve, numerically exact to ~1e-15, with a plain-NumPy fallback.

Note 05 · De-AmericanizationQuote preparation

American quotes are converted, not ignored — the tree is a control variate, not a pricing engine.

US-listed single-stock and ETF options are American: they can be exercised early, and their quotes embed an early-exercise premium that a European model does not know about. Fitting without removing it biases the put wing by whole vol points. The cure: invert each quote through a Cox–Ross–Rubinstein (CRR) binomial tree and keep the European-equivalent vol.

Per-strike CRR inversion — return the European-equivalent Black vol
{{EQ:deam}}
  • Escrowed-spot lattice handles discrete dividends; the early-exercise premium is removed strike by strike.
  • Compiled CRR kernel (Numba): ~60× on wide chains, plus a content-digest cache so re-tuning a fit never re-runs the conversion.
  • Caution, learned in production: a whole-curve convexity repair moved the dense ATM core and gapped live SPY/NVDA smiles — reverted; the shipped repair is wing-only, band-constrained, with the ATM core held exactly fixed.

Note 06 · Forwards & dividendsCase file inside

The forward comes from the chain — and the worst-identified parameter is clamped, not trusted.

Every implied vol depends on the forward F and the discount factor D used to compute it. Both are estimated from the option chain itself — put–call parity holds model-free, so a regression of C−P on the strike recovers them with no external curve. The catch: the regression slope (the discount) is poorly identified on noisy chains, and an error there drags the forward and tears the ATM smile.

Put–call parity as a regression — slope is the discount, level is the forward
{{EQ:parity}}
Case file — the delayed feed that gapped the ATM smile

A delayed data tier quoted stale pairs; the parity slope drifted to discounts above 1 (negative rates), dragging the forward and tearing a gap into the ATM smile. Fix: clamp the implied rate to a physical band and re-derive the forward from the well-identified price level. Clean chains are byte-identical; the broken feed heals.

  • Four dividend modes (yield, discrete cash, blends) + a de-bias loop that holds the discount fixed while removing American bias from the parity inputs.

Forwards workspace — live SPY (Yahoo feed). The forward curve with per-expiry parity-implied vs theoretical carry values, the source of each, manual overrides, and a click-to-edit discrete dividend schedule that feeds both the forward model and the de-Americanization lattice.

One var-swap quote pins the whole wing mass — entered as a soft target shared by every model.

A variance swap pays realized variance; its fair strike is a model-free integral over the whole smile, dominated by the wings. Desks often trust a dealer var-swap mark more than any individual deep-wing quote — so the app accepts one var-swap level per (name, expiry) and lets it discipline the wings of whichever model is fitting.

Note 09 · Wings & Lee boundsTail discipline

Wings obey moment logic — Lee's bound caps every model's tails, each by its own mechanism.

Lee's moment formula: total implied variance can grow at most linearly in |log-moneyness|, with slope at most 2, and the slope is tied to how many moments the terminal distribution has. A steeper wing implies an infinite price for some traded payoff — free money. It is the one universal law of smile extrapolation.

Lee's moment formula — asymptotic variance slopes capped at 2
{{EQ:lqd_tail}}
  • LQD: structural — the endpoint scales AL, AR imply the slopes; admissibility is one inequality.
  • SVI: a soft cap b(1+|ρ|) ≤ 2 in the penalty vocabulary.
  • MC-SIV: hats are wing-neutral by construction — the base alone owns the tails.
  • The far wing is where models extrapolate with confidence they don't have — so extrapolation behaviour is a stated, tested contract per model.

Tails, quantified. Left — Lee's ψ(p): the exact map from the number of finite moments to the admissible variance slope. Right — how each model extrapolates beyond the last quote; the differences are the point.

Trading relevance Deep OTM marks inherit tail discipline from moment theory — not from whatever the last two quotes happened to say.

Note 10 · Calendar arbitrageCase file

Constraints act only where data lives — a production incident made that a design rule.

Calendar arbitrage: if an option with more time is cheaper than one with less (same forward-moneyness), a calendar spread earns free convexity. The clean statement is in total variance w = σ²T, which must be non-decreasing in maturity. The subtlety is not the constraint — it is where you are allowed to check it.

Calendar consistency — convex order, four equivalent faces

Note 11 · Event variance clockEvents are time

Events are modeled as time, not as smile damage — the variance clock is price-preserving by construction.

An earnings date concentrates a known lump of variance into a single day — which is why implied vol "crushes" after the print. Modeled correctly, this is a change of clock: measure maturity in variance time τ, where an event day counts as 1 + N ordinary days, and the kink in the term structure disappears.

The dilated clock — each event adds N equivalent days of variance
{{EQ:event_clock}}
  • Total variance w(k) is clock-independent — prices never move when an event is inserted; only the working IV drops. The vol crush is a change of units, not of value.
  • One clock per node, shared by every view: fits, local vol, term structure, tables.
  • Auto-calibrate places events so event-time forward variance is flat — the calendar is inferred from the term structure, then editable.
  • Empty calendar ⇒ τ = t, byte-identical — the feature costs nothing when unused.

Term workspace — live SPY (Yahoo feed) with an event calendar. ATM vol and forward variance across the expiry ladder, two scheduled events (2 equivalent days each) in the editable calendar, the real-time / event-dilated clock toggle, event auto-calibration, and the per-expiry var-swap levels.

Note 12 · Spot–vol dynamicsRecalibration-free transport

When spot moves, the surface moves by rule, before recalibration — one transport realizes any SSR.

SSR = skew-stickiness ratio: how much ATM vol moves when spot moves, in units of the skew. The classic regimes are points on one dial — R = 0 "sticky-moneyness" (the smile rides with spot), R = 1 "sticky-strike" (vol per strike frozen), R = 2 "sticky-local-vol". One analytic transport realizes any value in between, instantly and without refitting.

The SSR transport — one formula spans sticky-strike to sticky-moneyness and beyond
{{EQ:ssr_transport}}
  • SSR is the dial: R = 0 sticky-moneyness, R = 1 sticky-strike, R = 2 sticky-local-vol — and anything between, from one analytic map.

Note 15 · Observation Kalman filterNoise vs news

A Kalman filter in handle space separates quote noise from genuine surface motion.

Distinct from the prior (which fills gaps within a fit), the filter works across time: it treats each calibration as a noisy measurement of the true handles, predicts today from yesterday's filtered state under the SSR transport, and blends the two by relative precision. The blend weights are the Kalman gains K ∈ [0,1]: K = 1 means "believe today fully", K = 0 means "today is noise".

Dark smiles move by innovation, not by copying liquid names.
