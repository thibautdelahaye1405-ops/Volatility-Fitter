\begin{abstract}
This is the map of the territory. The Vol-Fitter is an implied-volatility
surface fitter in the spirit of a professional vola-fitter, with one
differentiating capability: it \emph{extrapolates} sparse smile observations to
a full universe of smiles, across expiries \emph{and} assets, by propagating
signal through a graph whose nodes are smiles $(\text{underlying},T)$. This note
states the market and normalization conventions shared by the whole series,
walks the end-to-end compute pipeline (fetch $\to$ de-Americanize $\to$ forwards
$\to$ fit $\to$ views $\to$ graph), catalogues what the application can do, and
ends with a \emph{master hyperparameter atlas} cross-linked to the topic notes
that derive each knob. It is the front door: read it once, then any other note
in the series stands alone.
\end{abstract}

\section{What the application is}
\label{sec:what}

At its core the Vol-Fitter solves one problem repeatedly --- \emph{given noisy,
sparse option quotes for one expiry of one underlying, produce a smooth,
arbitrage-free implied-volatility smile} --- and then layers three ambitions on
top of it:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{A choice of models, all arbitrage-aware.} Four smile
representations share one calibration spine: the \emph{LQD} log-quantile-density
model (Note~01), \emph{SVI / SVI-JW} (Note~02), the \emph{Multi-Core SIV} sigmoid
family (Note~03), and a full \emph{piecewise-affine local-volatility} surface
(Note~04). The first three are slice parametrizations that are butterfly-free by
construction or by penalty; the fourth is a genuine local-volatility grid solving
the Dupire PDE.
\item \textbf{A professional desk workflow.} Real bid/ask handling, var-swap
targets, density-corrected weighting, event time dilation, dividend and
forward treatment, spot--vol dynamics, and a Bayesian prior-persistence machine
that decides, quote by quote, whether to trust the market or the saved prior.
\item \textbf{Graph smile-extrapolation.} The headline differentiator (Note~14):
a transported prior plus the lit-node calibration innovation are propagated
through an optimal-transport-regularized Bayesian graph to reconstruct the
\emph{dark} nodes nobody quoted.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{heuristic}
The unifying idea is that a volatility surface is not a curve to be drawn but a
\emph{family of probability laws} to be inferred --- one risk-neutral law per
$(\text{underlying},T)$ node --- subject to no-arbitrage couplings (butterfly
within a slice, calendar across expiries) and to a Bayesian prior that ties the
whole universe together. Every design choice in the app follows from taking that
sentence literally.
\end{heuristic}

\Cref{fig:guarantees} shows the two structural guarantees on a four-expiry
synthetic surface fitted by the production LQD calibrator: the implied densities
are all non-negative (no butterfly arbitrage) and the ATM total-variance term
structure is non-decreasing (no calendar arbitrage).

\section{Market conventions and normalization}
\label{sec:conventions}

These conventions hold across the entire series; individual notes assume them
without restating.

\begin{definition}[Forward measure and normalization]
For expiry $T$ with forward price $F_T$, work under the $T$-forward measure and
in forward-normalized, undiscounted units. The log-moneyness is
$k=\log(K/F_T)$, the log-forward return is $X_T=\log(S_T/F_T)$, and normalized
call/put prices are $C_T(k)=\E^T[(e^{X_T}-e^k)^+]$ and
$P_T(k)=\E^T[(e^k-e^{X_T})^+]$, with the martingale condition $\E^T[e^{X_T}]=1$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Total variance and the Black map]
Total implied variance is $w(k,T)=\sigma_{\BS}^2(k,T)\,T$. The normalized Black
call is $\Black(k,w)=\PhiN(d_+)-e^k\PhiN(d_-)$ with
$d_\pm=-k/\sqrt w\pm\sqrt w/2$. Implied total variance is the unique $w$ solving
$\Black(k,w)=C_T(k)$; the fitter inverts it by a vectorized scheme rather than a
scalar root-find per strike.
\end{definition}

Three conventions deserve emphasis because they recur:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \textbf{Two clocks.} Calendar time $t$ (year-fraction to expiry) and
\emph{variance} time $\tau$ (calendar time dilated by scheduled events) are kept
separate. Fits live on $\tau$; implied volatility is reported as $\sqrt{w/\tau}$,
so adding an event \emph{lowers} reported IV while preserving price (Note~11).
\item \textbf{Forward-normalized everything.} Prices, strikes and densities are
quoted relative to $F_T$, so a single smile object is spot-independent until a
dynamics rule (Note~12) transports it under a spot move.
\item \textbf{Vega-normalized residuals.} Calibration losses are price residuals
divided by Black vega, which makes the objective behave like a volatility error
while keeping every iterate a valid price (Notes~01, 04, 07).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Stage by stage.}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{Data feed.} A provider registry exposes Yahoo (US options),
Bloomberg (xbbg; US + non-US indices/equities), and Massive (Polygon/OPRA), plus
a synthetic source; an as-of selector serves Live / previous-close / EOD /
captured-replay. US-options-only providers and the non-US Bloomberg path are
handled transparently.
\item \textbf{De-Americanization} (Note~05). Listed equity options are American;
each quote is converted to its European-equivalent implied volatility by
inverting a Cox--Ross--Rubinstein tree (bisection), with a Numba kernel for wide
chains and a content-digest cache so fit-tuning never re-runs it.
\item \textbf{Forwards and dividends} (Note~06). Put--call parity yields the
implied forward and discount (clamped against feed noise); discrete dividends and
American de-bias are handled here.
\item \textbf{Prepare.} Out-of-the-money selection, a static no-arbitrage/wing
screen, and the choice of observation weights (Note~07).
\item \textbf{Calibrate slice.} The chosen model is fitted to the prepared quotes
under the active objective: mid / bid--ask band / haircut (Note~07), optional
var-swap target (Note~08), calendar coupling (Note~10), event clock (Note~11),
and Bayesian prior persistence (Note~13).
\item \textbf{Views.} Every smile-derived view --- smile, stacked densities,
stacked total variance, quantile density, term structure, local-vol surface,
SSR scenario --- follows the chosen model and is downsampled for the UI.
\item \textbf{Graph} (Note~14). The transported prior plus the lit-node
innovation propagate through the OT-regularized Bayesian graph to reconstruct
dark-node smiles with credible bands.
\end{enumerate}

\section{The smile graph}
\label{sec:graph}

The object that makes the app more than a slice fitter is the \emph{smile graph}
(\cref{fig:smilegraph}): nodes are smiles $(\text{underlying},T)$; edges encode
that one node informs another. Calendar edges link expiries of one name;
cross-asset edges link an index or sector ETF to its single names. A user marks
nodes \emph{lit} (quoted, calibrated) or \emph{dark} (to be reconstructed). The
graph carries the three ATM handles $(\sigma_0,s_0,\kappa_0)$ of each node as a
Gaussian signal and propagates the lit innovation to the dark nodes; Note~14
develops the optimal-transport-regularized Bayesian solver behind it, and the
leave-one-out backtest that measures its skill.

\section{How the notes are organized}
\label{sec:org}

Each note is standalone and follows the same skeleton: assumptions and heuristics
first, then full derivations in a clean-math body, then a worked example with
figures generated by the production code, and finally two appendices --- \textbf{A,
a hyperparameter atlas} (every knob, surfaced or hidden, with its default) and
\textbf{B, performance} (the numerical optimizations and measured timings). Notes
cross-reference each other (this note's \cref{tab:caps} is the index) but never
depend on one another's internals. Numbers and plots are \emph{measured}, not
copied: where a fresh run differs from an older design note, the fresh run wins.

\paragraph{Reading the atlas.} A surfaced parameter is a \emph{policy} choice ---
how strong a penalty, which prior mode, how to weight quotes --- safe to tune. A
hidden constant is tied to an algorithm's correctness (the $A_R$ buffer, the PDE
floor, the tree depth) and is exposed only in the relevant note's Appendix~A,
where the consequences of changing it are spelled out. Together they are the full
control surface of the fitter.
