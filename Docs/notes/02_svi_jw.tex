\begin{abstract}
This note documents the Vol-Fitter's SVI slice model. Raw SVI parametrizes total
implied variance as a hyperbola in log-moneyness,
$w(k)=a+b\{\rho(k-m)+\sqrt{(k-m)^2+\sigsvi^2}\}$, with five parameters that are
geometrically opaque but analytically convenient. The \emph{jump-wing} (SVI-JW)
re-coordinatization replaces them with five quantities in trader vocabulary ---
ATM variance, a time-scaled ATM skew, two normalized wing slopes, and the
minimum variance. Product status up front: JW is the analytical and benchmark
coordinate system of this note; production fits and stores \emph{raw} SVI and
displays model-agnostic ATM handles, and five-handle JW entry, bumping and
export are not yet product features. We give the exact conversion in both
directions together with its \emph{regular inverse domain}: on $v>0$, $p,c>0$,
$-p/2<\psi<c/2$, $\psi\ne0$, $\widetilde v<v$ the displayed formulas define
the unique nonsingular inverse --- while the image also contains the singular
stratum $\psi=0$ (ATM at the vertex, $\widetilde v=v$), attained by infinitely
many raw slices, because the handles omit ATM curvature and cannot identify
$(m,\sigsvi)$ there. The production converter carries \emph{no} guards, so the
domain is the caller's contract, stated precisely and now test-locked. We
restate the two \emph{coded} no-arbitrage fences in JW units --- Lee's cap
becomes $\max(p,c)\sqrt{v\tau}\le2$ and the minimum-variance condition is
$\widetilde v\ge0$ --- and are precise about what they guarantee: they are
finite-weight, adjustable penalties that do \emph{not} certify Durrleman
$g\ge0$ (a classical slice passing both while arbitrageable is test-locked).
The unconstrained reparametrization (softplus / $\tanh$ / $\exp$) lets a plain
Levenberg--Marquardt solver only ever propose \emph{structurally valid
hyperbolas}. As in the LQD note, the residual Jacobian is available in closed
form; a fresh run recovers a benchmark slice through a production
JW$\to$raw$\to$fit$\to$JW round trip to a fraction of a vol bp, and runs
\svispeedup$\times$ faster than the finite-difference Jacobian on that
microbenchmark.
\end{abstract}

\section{Product status, conventions, and why two parametrizations}
\label{sec:why}

\paragraph{Conventions.} Throughout, $k=\log(K/F_T)$ is log-moneyness and
$w(k)=\sigma_{\mathrm{imp}}^2(k)\,\tau_T$ total implied variance, where $T$
labels the expiry, $t_T$ is its calendar year fraction and $\tau_T$ is the
\emph{active variance time} --- calendar time dilated by scheduled events
(Notes~00, 11), with $\tau_T=t_T$ when the event clock is off. Production
passes $\tau_T$ (\texttt{prepared.tau}) to the SVI calibrator, not the plain
calendar maturity; classical formulas below write a bare $t$, and wherever it
plays a time role, read $\tau_T$. The raw SVI width parameter is written
$\sigsvi$ so it cannot be confused with an implied volatility. A
\emph{volatility basis point} (vol bp) is $10^{-4}$ in volatility. LM is the
Levenberg--Marquardt least-squares algorithm and \texttt{trf} scipy's
trust-region-reflective alternative; $P=5$ is the SVI parameter count wherever
per-parameter costs appear.

\paragraph{Product status.} JW is the trader-oriented \emph{analytical}
coordinate system used by this note and by the benchmark helper
(\texttt{jw\_to\_raw}, whose only repository caller is a benchmark test).
Current production calibration fits and stores \emph{raw} SVI as the selected
overlay family and displays three model-agnostic numeric handles --- ATM vol,
skew and curvature, by finite differences of the displayed curve --- for every
model. There is no backend \texttt{raw\_to\_jw}, no five-handle JW API/UI
entry, no JW bumping and no JW export; those are candidate features, not
shipped ones. Keep this in mind whenever the prose below says what a desk
``quotes'' or ``reads'': it describes the coordinate system, not a wired
workflow.

SVI is the workhorse of single-slice equity-index fitting because the hyperbola
\begin{equation}
w(k)=a+b\Big\{\rho(k-m)+\sqrt{(k-m)^2+\sigsvi^2}\Big\}
\label{eq:rawsvi}
\end{equation}
is, in five parameters, simultaneously (i) linear in its wings ---
$w(k)\sim b(1\pm\rho)\,|k|$ --- so its wing \emph{form} is Lee-compatible
whenever the slope cap of \cref{sec:noarb} holds, (ii) smooth and
convex enough to be butterfly-free over a wide admissible cone, and (iii) cheap
to evaluate and differentiate. The cost is that $(a,b,\rho,m,\sigsvi)$ are
geometrically entangled: no single one is ``the skew'' or ``the ATM level''. The
Vol-Fitter therefore carries SVI in \emph{two} coordinate systems --- raw for the
optimizer, jump-wing for the analysis --- and converts exactly between them on
the domain of \cref{sec:domain}.

\paragraph{Four tiers of ``valid''.} The words matter in this note, so we fix
them once:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{Structurally valid hyperbola}: $b>0$, $|\rho|<1$, $\sigsvi>0$.
Guaranteed for \emph{every} optimizer iterate by the reparametrization of
\cref{sec:calib} --- but note $a$ stays free, so an iterate can still have
negative total variance somewhere.
\item \textbf{Minimum/Lee clean}: $\min_k w(k)\ge0$ and $b(1+|\rho|)\le2$.
These are the two \emph{coded} penalties --- finite-weight, configurable, zero
on a clean slice.
\item \textbf{Butterfly clean}: $w>0$ and Durrleman $g(k)\ge0$ everywhere
(with the appropriate boundary behaviour). \emph{Not} implied by tier 2: the
counterexample of \cref{sec:noarb} passes both screens with $g<0$.
\item \textbf{Calendar clean}: the cross-expiry ordering
$w_{T_1}(k)\le w_{T_2}(k)$ holds; supplied by the model-agnostic hinge of
Note~10, again as a finite-weight penalty.
\end{enumerate}
Raw SVI slices can remain arbitrageable without conditions stronger than
tier~2 --- this is standard in the SVI literature (Gatheral--Jacquier; the
full butterfly-domain characterization is Martini--Mingone).

\begin{invariant}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item The optimizer can only propose structurally valid hyperbolas (tier 1):
$b\ge0$, $|\rho|<1$, $\sigsvi>0$ hold by reparametrization, not by rejection.
\item The two coded no-arbitrage fences (tier 2) --- non-negative minimum
variance and the Lee wing cap --- are soft hinges that are \emph{exactly zero}
on any tier-2-clean slice (byte-identical fits). They bound, but do not
certify, tier-3 butterfly cleanliness.
\item The raw$\leftrightarrow$JW conversion is exact on its \emph{regular}
domain (\cref{prop:domain}); the converter itself carries no guards, so the
domain is the caller's contract (test-locked).
\item SVI is a display overlay like any other: the band, calendar, var-swap,
prior and extrapolation-fence blocks share the same product targets as the
other models, expressed through \emph{model-specific} residuals (SVI works in
IV units; LQD in vega-normalized price; the var-swap routes differ). Each is
absent-is-byte-identical.
\end{enumerate}
\end{invariant}

\begin{heuristic}
Read \eqref{eq:rawsvi} as a hyperbola: $b$ is the overall steepness (the
asymptotic opening angle), $\rho\in(-1,1)$ tilts it (left wing steeper than right
for the equity put skew $\rho<0$), $m$ shifts its vertex in $k$, $\sigsvi$ rounds
off the vertex (larger $\sigsvi$ = flatter bottom), and $a$ raises the whole curve.
The wings are straight lines of slope $b(1-\rho)$ on the left and $b(1+\rho)$ on
the right --- which is exactly what Lee's bound constrains.
\end{heuristic}

\section{Raw SVI geometry}
\label{sec:raw}

The derivatives used everywhere below are
\begin{equation}
w'(k)=b\Big(\rho+\frac{k-m}{r}\Big),\qquad
w''(k)=\frac{b\,\sigsvi^2}{r^3},\qquad
r=\sqrt{(k-m)^2+\sigsvi^2}.
\label{eq:svi_deriv}
\end{equation}
Note $w''>0$ always (the slice is one globally convex curve in $k$, with a
single minimum --- the geometric fact behind the model's rigidity on event
shapes, \cref{sec:original}), and the wing limits are
\begin{equation}
\lim_{k\to-\infty}\frac{w(k)}{|k|}=b(1-\rho),
\qquad
\lim_{k\to+\infty}\frac{w(k)}{k}=b(1+\rho).
\label{eq:svi_wings}
\end{equation}
The vertex (minimum total variance) sits at $k^\star=m-\sigsvi\rho/\sqrt{1-\rho^2}$
with value $w(k^\star)=a+b\sigsvi\sqrt{1-\rho^2}$; this minimum is the quantity the
jump-wing parametrization calls $\widetilde v\,t$.

\section{The jump-wing handles}
\label{sec:jw}

The SVI-JW parametrization $(v,\psi,p,c,\widetilde v)$ at expiry $t$ is defined by
ATM and wing functionals of the slice:
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
v&=\frac{w(0)}{t} && \text{ATM variance,}\\
\psi&=\frac{b}{2\sqrt{w_0}}\Big(\rho-\frac{m}{\sqrt{m^2+\sigsvi^2}}\Big) && \text{ATM skew (time-scaled),}\\
p&=\frac{b(1-\rho)}{\sqrt{w_0}},\quad c=\frac{b(1+\rho)}{\sqrt{w_0}} && \text{put/call wing slopes (normalized),}\\
\widetilde v&=\frac{a+b\sigsvi\sqrt{1-\rho^2}}{t} && \text{minimum variance,}
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:jw_def}
\end{equation}
with $w_0=w(0)=vt$.

\subsection{What the handles are, in displayed units}
\label{sec:jw_units}

None of the five handles is a directly plotted quantity, and misreading their
units is the most common JW error. At the production variance time $\tau$:
\begin{equation}
\sigma_{\text{ATM}}=\sqrt v,\qquad
\partial_k\sigma_{\text{imp}}(0)=\frac{\psi}{\sqrt\tau},\qquad
\beta_L=p\sqrt{v\tau},\qquad
\beta_R=c\sqrt{v\tau},\qquad
\sigma_{\min}=\sqrt{\widetilde v},
\label{eq:jw_units}
\end{equation}
where $\beta_{L,R}$ are the asymptotic \emph{total-variance} slopes
$b(1\mp\rho)$ of \eqref{eq:svi_wings}. In words: $v$ and $\widetilde v$ are
\emph{variances} (the IV chart shows their square roots); $\psi$ is a
\emph{time-scaled skew handle}, not the displayed IV tangent --- the actual ATM
tangent slope is $\psi/\sqrt\tau$; and $p,c$ are \emph{normalized
total-variance wing slopes}, not slopes of the IV curve. \Cref{fig:jw}
annotates a fitted slice in exactly these displayed units, with the wing
slopes on a total-variance inset where they actually live.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.86\textwidth]{fig_svi_jw.pdf}
\caption{The five SVI-JW handles in \emph{displayed} units. Main panel (IV):
the ATM level $\sqrt v$, the minimum $\sqrt{\widetilde v}$, and the ATM
tangent of slope $\psi/\sqrt\tau$. Inset (total variance $w(k)$): the two
asymptotes whose slopes are $\beta_L=p\sqrt{v\tau}$ and

\subsection{The conversion, both ways}

The forward direction, jump-wing \emph{to} raw, is what the shipped converter
\texttt{jw\_to\_raw} computes (today its only repository caller is the
benchmark test; see the product-status paragraph of \cref{sec:why}). Set
$w_0=vt$, $\sqrt{w_0}=\sqrt{vt}$, and
\begin{equation}
b=\frac{\sqrt{w_0}}{2}(p+c),\qquad \rho=\frac{c-p}{c+p},\qquad
\chi:=\frac{m}{\sqrt{m^2+\sigsvi^2}}=\rho-\frac{4\psi}{p+c}.
\label{eq:jw_to_raw_1}
\end{equation}
From $\chi$ one recovers $m=\chi\sigsvi/\sqrt{1-\chi^2}$ and
$\sqrt{m^2+\sigsvi^2}=\sigsvi/\sqrt{1-\chi^2}$; subtracting the minimum-variance
identity from the ATM identity gives the closed form
\begin{equation}
\sigsvi=\frac{w_0-\widetilde v\,t}{b\big((1-\rho\chi)/\sqrt{1-\chi^2}-\sqrt{1-\rho^2}\big)},
\qquad a=\widetilde v\,t-b\sigsvi\sqrt{1-\rho^2}.
\label{eq:jw_to_raw_2}
\end{equation}
The reverse map \emph{from} raw \emph{to} jump-wing is just the definitions
\eqref{eq:jw_def} evaluated on the fitted raw slice; the backend ships no
\texttt{raw\_to\_jw} --- that direction lives in the figure generator and in
these equations. \Cref{sec:example} runs the production round trip
numerically. The forward conversion
\eqref{eq:jw_to_raw_1}--\eqref{eq:jw_to_raw_2} is direct:

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={SVI-JW handles $\to$ raw SVI (distilled from
\texttt{models/svi\_jw/svi.py}; agrees with it to $10^{-12}$).},label={lst:jw2raw}]
def jw_to_raw(t, v, psi, p, c, vt):
    # (v, psi, p, c, vtilde) -> (a, b, rho, m, sigma)
    w0 = v*t; sq = np.sqrt(w0)
    b = 0.5*sq*(p + c)
    rho = (c - p)/(c + p)
    chi = rho - 4*psi/(p + c)      # m / sqrt(m^2 + sigma^2)
    r1x = np.sqrt(1 - chi*chi)
    r1r = np.sqrt(1 - rho*rho)
    sigma = (w0 - vt*t) / (b*((1 - rho*chi)/r1x - r1r))
    m = chi*sigma/r1x
    a = vt*t - b*sigma*r1r
    return a, b, rho, m, sigma
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{The regular inverse domain, exactly}
\label{sec:domain}

Not every quintuple $(v,\psi,p,c,\widetilde v)$ is a smile, and --- a finer
point --- \emph{regular invertibility} is not the same as
\emph{representability}. The formulas above divide by $p+c$, by
$\sqrt{1-\chi^2}$ and by the bracket in \eqref{eq:jw_to_raw_2}, and the
production converter carries \emph{no} guards --- so the exact domain is worth
stating and proving.

\begin{proposition}[Regular inverse domain]
\label{prop:domain}
Fix $t>0$. On
\[
v>0,\qquad p>0,\ c>0,\qquad -\frac{p}{2}<\psi<\frac{c}{2},\qquad
\psi\neq0,\qquad \widetilde v<v ,
\]
the map \eqref{eq:jw_to_raw_1}--\eqref{eq:jw_to_raw_2} defines the unique,
nonsingular inverse of the JW definitions \eqref{eq:jw_def}: it produces a
structurally valid raw slice ($b>0$, $|\rho|<1$, $\sigsvi>0$, $m$ finite), and
outside this set the displayed formulas produce no such slice. In words: the
wings must be genuinely upward, the quoted ATM skew must lie strictly between
\emph{half the wing slopes}, and the ATM variance must sit strictly above the
minimum.
\end{proposition}

Two complements make the full picture. First, \emph{representability} is
larger than regular invertibility: the image of the JW map also contains the
singular stratum $\psi=0$, $\widetilde v=v$ (\cref{rem:psi0} below), realized
by \emph{infinitely many} raw slices --- so ``the formulas fail'' does not
mean ``no slice has these handles''. Second, a financially usable smile
additionally requires $\widetilde v\ge0$ (tier 2 of \cref{sec:why}); that is
an economic condition, separate from the algebraic invertibility above.

\begin{proof}
$p,c>0$ gives $b=\tfrac{\sqrt{w_0}}{2}(p+c)>0$ and
$\rho=(c-p)/(c+p)\in(-1,1)$. For $\chi=\rho-4\psi/(p+c)$, using
$(\rho-1)(p+c)=-2p$ and $(\rho+1)(p+c)=2c$,
\[
|\chi|<1
\iff \frac{(\rho-1)(p+c)}{4}<\psi<\frac{(\rho+1)(p+c)}{4}
\iff -\frac p2<\psi<\frac c2 ,
\]
which keeps $m=\chi\sigsvi/\sqrt{1-\chi^2}$ finite. For $\sigsvi$: by
Cauchy--Schwarz applied to the unit vectors $(\rho,\sqrt{1-\rho^2})$ and
$(\chi,\sqrt{1-\chi^2})$,
\[
\rho\chi+\sqrt{1-\rho^2}\sqrt{1-\chi^2}\le1,
\quad\text{i.e.}\quad
\frac{1-\rho\chi}{\sqrt{1-\chi^2}}\ \ge\ \sqrt{1-\rho^2},
\]
with equality iff $\chi=\rho$, i.e.\ iff $\psi=0$. So the bracket in
\eqref{eq:jw_to_raw_2} is strictly positive exactly when $\psi\neq0$, and
$\sigsvi>0$ then requires the numerator $w_0-\widetilde vt>0$, i.e.\
$\widetilde v<v$. Conversely each failure breaks a requirement. If $p+c<0$
then $b<0$; if $p+c=0$ the very first formula is undefined; and if $p+c>0$
with one of $p,c\le0$ then $|\rho|=|c-p|/(c+p)\ge1$ --- so $p,c>0$ is forced.
$\psi$ outside the band sends $|\chi|\ge1$ ($m$ infinite); $\psi=0$ zeroes
the bracket; $\widetilde v\ge v$ makes $\sigsvi\le0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[The $\psi=0$ stratum is a genuine coordinate singularity]
\label{rem:psi0}
On a true SVI slice, $\psi=0$ means $\chi=\rho$, which happens exactly when
the ATM point \emph{is} the vertex --- and then $\widetilde v=v$
automatically. (Call it the \emph{ATM-at-the-vertex} stratum: $k^\star=0$
implies no symmetry unless $\rho=0$.) On that stratum the five handles do not
identify the slice: for any $\sigsvi>0$, the raw slice with
$m=\rho\sigsvi/\sqrt{1-\rho^2}$ and $a=\widetilde vt-b\sigsvi\sqrt{1-\rho^2}$
reproduces the \emph{same} $(v,0,p,c,v)$ --- the handles omit ATM curvature,
and this is where it shows (test-locked:
\texttt{test\_svi\_domain.py::test\_psi\_zero\_stratum\_not\_identified}).
The jump-wing chart is a fine coordinate system \emph{away} from the
ATM-at-the-vertex stratum, and ill-conditioned near it: as $\psi\to0$, the
conversion computes $\sigsvi$ as a ratio of two vanishing quantities, with
catastrophic cancellation before the limit.
\end{remark}

\begin{caution}
The production \texttt{jw\_to\_raw} implements the formulas verbatim with
\emph{zero} domain checks, and the failure modes differ by case --- none is
uniformly ``silent'': a scalar $p+c=0$ raises \texttt{ZeroDivisionError};
$\psi$ outside $(-p/2,c/2)$ takes the square root of a negative and returns
NaNs (with a runtime warning); $\widetilde v\ge v$ returns $\sigsvi\le0$, not
a slice; and a near-zero $\psi$ silently amplifies rounding. All of this is
deliberate --- the converter's only caller feeds it benchmark handles
comfortably inside \cref{prop:domain} --- but it makes the domain a
\emph{contract}, not a runtime guarantee. The contract and each failure mode
are now test-locked (\texttt{test\_svi\_domain.py}); anyone wiring JW handles
to user input must still validate against \cref{prop:domain} first.
\end{caution}

\section{Static no-arbitrage}
\label{sec:noarb}

\subsection{Butterfly: Durrleman's function}

For a positive, sufficiently smooth total-variance slice $w$ with the
appropriate wing behaviour (the large-$|k|$ limits that make the density
integrate to one --- satisfied by any structurally valid SVI slice with
$w>0$), the slice is free of butterfly arbitrage iff the risk-neutral density
it implies is non-negative, which Durrleman's condition expresses directly on
total variance:
\begin{equation}
g(k)=\Big(1-\frac{k\,w'(k)}{2w(k)}\Big)^2-\frac{w'(k)^2}{4}\Big(\frac{1}{w(k)}+\frac14\Big)+\frac{w''(k)}{2}\ \ge\ 0
\quad\forall k.
\label{eq:durrleman}
\end{equation}
For raw SVI, $g$ is a smooth function of \eqref{eq:rawsvi}--\eqref{eq:svi_deriv}
with no closed-form sign, and where each consumer evaluates it matters:
the backtest's arbitrage metric evaluates the closed-form $(w,w',w'')$ triple
(Note~09); the application's extrapolated-region diagnostic finite-differences
the smooth \emph{model} $w$ (never reconstructed option prices); this note's
figures use the closed form. The \emph{default} SVI fit itself carries no $g$
hinge and samples no $g$ rows --- the Gatheral--Jacquier parameter-space
conditions bound the wing steepness $b(1+|\rho|)$ and the minimum total
variance, and the calibrator's core fences are the two that usually bite.

That ``usually'' is load-bearing, and there is a classical counterexample:
the Gatheral--Jacquier slice
$(a,b,\rho,m,\sigsvi)=(-0.0410,\,0.1331,\,0.3060,\,0.3586,\,0.4153)$ has
positive minimum variance ($0.0116$) and a Lee slope far under the cap
($0.174$), yet $g(k)$ dips to $-0.033$ near $k\approx0.88$: both coded screens
pass while genuine butterfly arbitrage remains (test-locked in
\texttt{test\_svi\_domain.py}; full anchor in the traceability table).

How $g$ is \emph{measured} matters as much as what it says. An earlier
backtest metric evaluated $g$ by finite differences of reconstructed option
prices, and its numerical noise flagged $28.3\%$ of provably-clean LQD slices
as arbitrageable; recomputed analytically from each model's own
$(w,w',w'')$, the LQD rate reads $0.0\%$, and SVI's flagged rate fell from
$20.8\%$ to $9.2\%$ --- the survivors being genuine: the soft hinges fence
the conditions that bite, they do not certify $g\ge0$ pointwise. The
production rule that came out of that audit is that the diagnostic is always
computed on model derivatives, never by differencing prices (Note~09 has the
cross-model treatment).

\subsection{The four production layers}
\label{sec:layers}

What production actually runs is a stack of four distinct layers, in
increasing distance from the core fit:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{Core fences} (always in the objective): the min-variance and
Lee penalty rows of \cref{sec:penalties} --- finite-weight, configurable,
zero on a clean slice.
\item \textbf{Extrapolated-region enforcement} (\texttt{extrapEnforce},
default \emph{off}; Notes 09--10): opt-in tapered residual rows on the
extrapolated strike region --- a Durrleman-$g$ hinge on the slice's own
envelope, a tapered calendar hinge against the previous displayed slice, and
the wing-slope-order hinge. These rows are finite-differenced while the core
SVI rows stay analytic, so the fit runs a \emph{hybrid} Jacobian
(\texttt{calib/extrap.py}; \texttt{models/svi\_jw/calibrate.py}).
\item \textbf{Advisory diagnostics} (always on): the Quality tab measures and
reports remaining extrapolated-region $g<0$ per fit --- measurement, not
enforcement.
\item \textbf{Publish-time wing projection} (export-only; Note 09 Phase 3): a
discrete projection of the exported wing samples that raises prices only,
leaving the fitted core pinned.
\end{enumerate}
None of these layers turns a fitted SVI slice into a certified butterfly-free
object; together they bound, measure, and clean at the boundary where it
matters.

\begin{proposition}[The enforced conditions, in JW units]
\label{prop:jwnoarb}
Under the conversion of \cref{sec:jw}, the two coded penalties read directly
on the trader handles: the minimum-variance condition
$a+b\sigsvi\sqrt{1-\rho^2}\ge0$ is exactly $\widetilde v\ge0$, and Lee's cap
$b(1+|\rho|)\le\beta_{\max}$ is exactly
\[
\max(p,c)\,\sqrt{vt}\ \le\ \beta_{\max}\ (=2).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The first is the definition of $\widetilde v$ in \eqref{eq:jw_def} times
$t>0$. For the second, $b(1\mp\rho)=p\sqrt{w_0}$ and $c\sqrt{w_0}$
respectively by \eqref{eq:jw_def}, so
$b(1+|\rho|)=\max\big(b(1-\rho),b(1+\rho)\big)=\max(p,c)\sqrt{w_0}$ with
$w_0=vt$.
\end{proof}

So a desk can sanity-check a handle quote before any conversion: wings
non-negative at the vertex ($\widetilde v\ge0$), skew inside
$(-p/2,\,c/2)$ (\cref{prop:domain}), and $\max(p,c)\sqrt{vt}\le2$ (Lee).

\subsection{The two soft penalties}
\label{sec:penalties}

The optimizer adds, to the data residual, two penalty rows that are
\emph{exactly zero} on a tier-2-clean slice and grow linearly past the
boundary:
\begin{equation}
\rho_{\text{pen}}(\theta)=W\!\begin{pmatrix}
\max\big(-(a+b\sigsvi\sqrt{1-\rho^2}),\,0\big)\\[2pt]
\max\big(b(1+|\rho|)-\beta_{\max},\,0\big)
\end{pmatrix},
\label{eq:svi_penalties}
\end{equation}
with residual multiplier $W=\svipenalty$ and Lee cap
$\beta_{\max}=\svileemax$. The first prevents \emph{negative total variance at
the minimum}; the second enforces Lee's bound that the asymptotic
total-variance slope cannot exceed $2$. Because both vanish on a clean fit, a
tier-2-clean smile (such as the benchmark of \cref{sec:example}) is
byte-identical with or without them --- they are a feasibility fence, not a
regularizer.

\begin{caution}
Read the semantics of $W$ carefully: it multiplies the \emph{residual} rows in
\eqref{eq:svi_penalties}, so the least-squares objective receives an effective
$W^2$ coefficient on the squared violation --- and the two rows do not even
share a natural financial unit (one is a total variance, the other a
dimensionless slope), so $W$ is a residual multiplier, not a ``weight in
vol$^2$ units''. It is deliberately large enough to dominate a violated
constraint yet invisible on a clean slice. It is \emph{not} a tuning dial for
fit quality: raising it cannot improve a feasible fit, and lowering it only
lets the optimizer wander into the arbitrageable cone. Both fences are also
\emph{adjustable}, not unconditional: a user can set
\texttt{sviPenaltyWeight}$\,=0$ (fences off) or pick a \texttt{leeSlopeMax}
above $2$, so they are configuration, not guarantees. Note~09 develops the
Lee bound across all the models; here it is one of these two hinges.
\end{caution}

%======================================================================
\section{Calibration}
\label{sec:calib}

\subsection{Unconstrained reparametrization}

Rather than run a bound-constrained optimizer, the Vol-Fitter reparametrizes so
that \emph{every} point of $\R^5$ maps to a \emph{structurally valid raw
hyperbola} (tier 1 of \cref{sec:why}):
\begin{equation}
a\ \text{free},\quad
b=\operatorname{softplus}(\theta_b)\ge 0,\quad
\rho=\tanh(\theta_\rho)\in(-1,1),\quad
m\ \text{free},\quad
\sigsvi=e^{\theta_\sigma}>0.
\label{eq:reparam}
\end{equation}
This guarantees $b\ge0$, $|\rho|<1$, $\sigsvi>0$ for free, so the unconstrained
Levenberg--Marquardt solver never wastes effort on the box and never proposes a
nonsensical hyperbola. It guarantees no more than that: $a$ stays free, so an
optimizer iterate can still carry negative total variance somewhere (the vol
conversion floors $w$ at $10^{-12}$ so the residual stays evaluable), and
arbitrage cleanliness is the business of the fences of \cref{sec:noarb}, which
remain soft.

\subsection{Objective, weights and the data-driven start}

The data term is a plain implied-volatility residual,
$r_i=\sqrt{\omega_i}\,(\sigma^{\text{SVI}}(k_i)-\sigma_i)$. The surfaced
weighting choices are \texttt{weightScheme} $\in$ \{\texttt{equal},
\texttt{tv\_density}\} (Note~07) --- the same two schemes every model gets;
the calibrator's low-level API also accepts arbitrary $\omega_i$ (e.g.\
vega$^2$, used by tests and research scripts), but that is a code capability,
not a surfaced setting. The initializer is geometric and reads the smile
directly: the argmin of $w$ seeds $(a,m)$, and the two finite wing slopes seed
$(b,\rho)$ via \eqref{eq:svi_wings}, then the reparametrization
\eqref{eq:reparam} is inverted to get $\theta_0$. On a liquid smile a single LM
pass converges from this start.

\subsection{The analytic Jacobian, derived}
\label{sec:jacobian}

The analytic Jacobian is worth deriving rather than asserting; it is three
chain-rule layers stacked (\texttt{models/svi\_jw/jacobian.py}). With
$x=k-m$ and $r=\sqrt{x^2+\sigsvi^2}$, the raw-parameter partials of the
hyperbola are
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial w}{\partial a}=1,\quad
\frac{\partial w}{\partial b}=\rho x+r,\quad
\frac{\partial w}{\partial\rho}=b\,x,\quad
\frac{\partial w}{\partial m}=-b\Big(\rho+\frac{x}{r}\Big),\quad
\frac{\partial w}{\partial\sigsvi}=\frac{b\,\sigsvi}{r}.
\label{eq:svi_partials}
\end{equation}
The data rows live in IV units, so the second layer is the Black chain rule
for $\sigma_{\text{imp}}=\sqrt{w/\tau}$:
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial\sigma_{\text{imp}}}{\partial w}
=\frac{1}{2\sqrt{w\,\tau}}
=\frac{1}{2\,\tau\,\sigma_{\text{imp}}},
\label{eq:iv_chain}
\end{equation}
with the gradient zeroed wherever the $w\le10^{-12}$ floor is active (the
clamp flattens it). The third layer is the reparametrization
\eqref{eq:reparam}:
\begin{equation}
\frac{\dd b}{\dd\theta_b}=\operatorname{sigmoid}(\theta_b)=1-e^{-b},\qquad
\frac{\dd\rho}{\dd\theta_\rho}=1-\rho^2,\qquad
\frac{\dd\sigsvi}{\dd\theta_\sigma}=\sigsvi.
\label{eq:reparam_chain}
\end{equation}
The hinge rows use the subgradient convention ``active linear part, else
zero'': a penalty/band/calendar $\max(\cdot,0)$ row contributes its full
linear derivative where the violation is strictly positive and a zero row
otherwise (at the kink itself the row is treated as inactive). Concretely,
the min-variance row differentiates
$-(a+b\sigsvi\sqrt{1-\rho^2})$ --- e.g.\ its $\theta_\rho$ entry is
$+Wb\sigsvi\rho\sqrt{1-\rho^2}$ after \eqref{eq:reparam_chain} --- and the
Lee row differentiates $b(1+|\rho|)$ with the $\operatorname{sign}(\rho)$
subgradient for $|\rho|$. The calibrator uses this Jacobian whenever no
var-swap or prior block is present; those blocks are not differentiated and
switch the fit to scipy's finite differences, exactly as in LQD. With
\texttt{extrapEnforce} on, the fit is \emph{hybrid}: the rows above stay
analytic and only the small extrapolation block is finite-differenced.

\subsection{The optional blocks}

The optional blocks share the same product targets as the rest of the series,
expressed through model-specific residuals:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \textbf{Band fit} (Note~07): the mid residual becomes a vol-space band
hinge with a small mid anchor.
\item \textbf{Calendar hinge} (Note~10): the \emph{model-agnostic} constraint
$\sqrt{W_{\text{cal}}}\,\max(\text{floor}-w(k),0)$ penalizes calendar
crossings against the nearer expiry --- the same product target LQD expresses
via its quantile $A(z)$, here written directly on $w(k)$. Being a
finite-weight hinge, it drives crossings down rather than provably to zero;
the locking tests require a substantial reduction of the violation, not exact
elimination.
\item \textbf{Extrapolation fences} (\texttt{extrapEnforce}, Notes 09--10):
the tapered extrapolated-region rows of \cref{sec:layers}, default off,
hybrid-Jacobian when on.
\item \textbf{Var-swap target} (Note~08) and \textbf{prior persistence}
(Note~13): the same targets as LQD through SVI-native residuals (the var-swap
routes differ per model: LQD uses its closed-form quantile moment, SVI the
strike replication) --- so the SVI \emph{display overlay} receives the same
prior treatment as the primary model, closing a long-standing asymmetry in
which SVI overlays got no prior at all.
\end{itemize}

\begin{perfbox}
The solver is Levenberg--Marquardt (\texttt{method="lm"}), not trust-region
reflective. On noisy real chains LM crosses the penalty kinks in far fewer
iterations than \texttt{trf} at matched tolerance, so it is the faster
same-accuracy choice; the analytic Jacobian is then a drop-in that swaps scipy's
$1+P$ finite-difference evaluations ($P=5$) for one closed-form call. It
shipped after the offline backtest (project \texttt{backtest/}) identified
those finite-difference evaluations as SVI's dominant fit cost. Two
measurements, labeled for what they are: (i) a \emph{historical real-node}
result --- $26.3\to10.2$\,ms per fit ($\approx2.6\times$) on spike-regime
backtest nodes, June-2026 harness configuration, same optimizer and
tolerances, same solution within fit precision; and (ii) this note's
\emph{private residual/Jacobian microbenchmark} --- \svispeedup$\times$ on the
25-quote synthetic benchmark (\cref{sec:example}), single-threaded on the
development machine, core rows only, costs agreeing to $\svicostdiff$. See
\cref{app:perf}.
\end{perfbox}

\section{What is original here, and limitations}
\label{sec:original}

SVI and the jump-wing handles are due to Gatheral; the arbitrage-free analysis is
Gatheral--Jacquier and Durrleman. The implementation choices worth noting are the
\emph{structural-validity-by-reparametrization} \eqref{eq:reparam} (the solver
cannot leave tier 1 except through the two soft economic fences), the
\emph{exact raw$\leftrightarrow$JW conversion with a proved regular domain}
(the analytical spine of this note and the benchmark; not yet a wired trader
workflow --- see \cref{sec:why}), and the \emph{analytic LM Jacobian} that
makes SVI the speed peer of the other models.

Limitations, stated plainly. Raw SVI total variance is \emph{one globally
convex hyperbola with a single minimum}, so it cannot reproduce a WW-shaped
total-variance smile (Note~03) and can be too rigid for event-shaped targets
(Note~01's double-hat); its single global curve can also over-smooth a sharp
short-dated skew. It is butterfly-clean only over a cone the coded fences do
not fully certify (\cref{sec:noarb}), and carries no calendar coupling on its
own --- that is supplied, softly, by the model-agnostic hinge of Note~10. On
sparse chains two further cautions apply: the initializer's ``wing slopes''
are finite-span proxies read off the quoted range, not asymptotic
observations, and a narrow or one-sided quote set can leave the raw
parameters poorly identified --- distinct $(a,b,\rho,m,\sigsvi)$ pricing the
quoted range indistinguishably --- even when the fitted \emph{curve} is
stable. Compare curves and handles across fits, not raw coefficients.

\section{Traceability}

A word on what these tests do and do not prove, so the anchors are not
over-read. The benchmark-fit tests exercise an \emph{already tier-2-clean}

\section{Performance notes}
\label{app:perf}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{Levenberg--Marquardt over trust-region.} On real noisy chains LM
crosses the penalty kinks in fewer iterations than \texttt{trf} at matched
tolerance; \texttt{trf} at $10^{-10}$ was measured \emph{slower} on real nodes.
\item \textbf{Analytic Jacobian.} Closed-form Jacobian of the core residual
stack (data $+$ penalties $+$ calendar) through the reparametrization. Two
measurements, with their scopes: the note's \emph{residual/Jacobian
microbenchmark} (25 synthetic quotes, core rows only, single-threaded
development machine, fastest of three) measured \svianalyticms\,ms vs
\svifdms\,ms (\svispeedup$\times$) with the costs agreeing to $\svicostdiff$
--- same solution within fit precision, not bit-identical; the
\emph{historical real-node} measurement (spike-regime backtest nodes,
June-2026 harness) was $26.3\to10.2$\,ms per fit ($\approx2.6\times$) --- the
finding that motivated shipping it. Gated to the var-swap/prior-free
configuration; hybrid (analytic core $+$ FD extrapolation rows) under
\texttt{extrapEnforce}.
\item \textbf{Structural validity by construction.} The reparametrization
removes the box-constraint handling entirely, so each LM step is a plain
linear solve.
\item \textbf{Geometric warm start.} Reading $(a,m)$ from the vertex and
$(b,\rho)$ from the wing slopes lands $\theta_0$ close enough that liquid smiles
converge in a single pass.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Reference implementation: reverse map and Jacobian core}
\label{app:ref}

The raw slice evaluates in one line,
$w(k)=a+b(\rho(k-m)+\sqrt{(k-m)^2+\sigsvi^2})$; the reverse conversion
\eqref{eq:jw_def} reads the five jump-wing handles off it. Composing with
\cref{lst:jw2raw} recovers the original handles to $10^{-10}$.

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={The reverse map raw $\to$ SVI-JW --- equations
\eqref{eq:jw_def}. The backend ships only the forward converter; this reverse
listing is the one the figure generator runs.}]
def w_svi(k, a, b, rho, m, sig):
    # the hyperbola w(k); sig is the SVI width
    km = k - m
    return a + b*(rho*km + np.sqrt(km*km + sig**2))

def raw_to_jw(t, a, b, rho, m, sig):
    # (a, b, rho, m, sig) -> (v, psi, p, c, vtilde)
    w0 = w_svi(0.0, a, b, rho, m, sig); sq = np.sqrt(w0)
    v = w0/t
    psi = b/(2*sq)*(rho - m/np.sqrt(m*m + sig**2))
    p, c = b*(1 - rho)/sq, b*(1 + rho)/sq
    vt = (a + b*sig*np.sqrt(1 - rho*rho))/t
    return v, psi, p, c, vt
\end{lstlisting}

The production Jacobian core (\cref{sec:jacobian}) is equally short --- the
raw partials \eqref{eq:svi_partials} times the reparametrization factors
\eqref{eq:reparam_chain}, then the IV chain rule \eqref{eq:iv_chain} for the
data rows (distilled from \texttt{models/svi\_jw/jacobian.py}, which it
matches to machine precision):

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={The analytic Jacobian's core: $\dd w/\dd\theta$
after the chain rule, and the IV data rows.}]
def dw_dtheta(k, a, b, rho, m, sig):
    # d w / d theta, theta = (a, th_b, th_rho, m, th_sig)
    x = k - m
    r = np.sqrt(x*x + sig*sig)
    sig_b = 1.0 - np.exp(-b)       # d softplus / d th_b
    J = np.empty((k.size, 5))
    J[:, 0] = 1.0                  # d/da
    J[:, 1] = (rho*x + r)*sig_b    # d/db . db/dth_b
    J[:, 2] = (b*x)*(1 - rho*rho)  # d/drho . drho/dth_rho
    J[:, 3] = -b*(rho + x/r)       # d/dm
    J[:, 4] = (b*sig/r)*sig        # d/dsig . dsig/dth_sig
    return J

def data_rows(k, t, w, J, sqrt_weights):
    # residual = sqrt(w_i) (sqrt(w(k)/t) - vol_i): chain rule
    model_vol = np.sqrt(np.maximum(w, 1e-12)/t)
    rows = J / (2.0*t*model_vol)[:, None]
    rows[w <= 1e-12] = 0.0         # the floor flattens the gradient
    return sqrt_weights[:, None] * rows
\end{lstlisting}
