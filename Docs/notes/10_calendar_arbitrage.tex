\begin{abstract}
Butterfly-freedom keeps a single smile a valid density; calendar-freedom
keeps a \emph{surface} consistent across maturities. This note states
precisely what that means and how the Vol-Fitter enforces it. Under
fixed-forward normalization with deterministic carry, calendar-freedom is
four equivalent statements --- normalized calls non-decreasing in maturity,
the terminal laws increasing in convex order, the integrated upper-quantile
curves ordered, and total implied variance non-decreasing at every fixed
log-moneyness --- and, by Kellerer's theorem, exactly the condition for the
fitted surface to be consistent with \emph{some} martingale. Two
enforcements follow. For LQD the integrated upper-quantile curve \emph{is}
the asset-share integral every built slice already carries, so the
constraint is an elementwise array comparison; expiries are fitted
nearest-to-farthest under a soft slack. For the SVI and Multi-Core Sigmoid
(MCS) overlays a \emph{model-agnostic} hinge enforces
$w_{\text{far}}(k)\ge w_{\text{near}}(k)$ directly (the local-vol surface
needs neither: positivity of its local variance is calendar-cleanliness,
Note~04). The note's case file is the \emph{phantom calendar}: a fixed wide
floor grid let a steep short expiry's linear-wing extrapolation manufacture
violations in a no-data region, flattening far fits live on NVDA and SPY;
the confinement fix --- sample the floor only on the traded range --- is
reproduced here with the production calibrator (far-fit error
\calphantomwide\ vol bp under the wide grid vs \calphantomdata\ under the
confined one). An explicitly-flagged open problem records what confinement
gives up: softer enforcement in the extrapolated region, where options are
not yet worthless (\cref{rem:openwings}). A worked example takes an
inverted-wing pair from a violation of \calviolfree\ to \calviolcured.
\end{abstract}

\section{What calendar-freedom means}
\label{sec:meaning}

\begin{assumption}[Units and carry]
\label{ass:units}
Each expiry is normalized by its own forward: $Y_T=S_T/F_T$ (so
$\E[Y_T]=1$ for every $T$), quotes live at fixed log-moneyness
$k=\log(K/F_T)$, and rates, borrow and dividends are deterministic and
absorbed into $F_T$ (Note~06). Comparisons across maturity are made at fixed
$k$ --- fixed \emph{forward} moneyness, not fixed cash strike. Discrete cash
dividends make the cash-strike statement fail while the normalized one
survives; stochastic rates would break the equivalence between calendar
spreads and the normalized comparison altogether. The event clock of
Note~11 reparametrizes maturity within each slice but preserves the
\emph{ordering} of expiries, so the statements below are unaffected.
\end{assumption}

\begin{invariant}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item Where data lives, the fitted surface is increasing in convex order:
no calendar spread against it.
\item Enforcement is a \emph{soft} hinge --- quotes still win a genuine
conflict, and the diagnostic reports what slack was used.
\item \texttt{enforceCalendar} off, or no previous slice, is byte-identical
to independent fits.
\item The constraint is sampled only where \emph{both} slices are pinned by
data --- the confinement principle (Note~09).
\end{enumerate}
\end{invariant}

\begin{theorem}[Four faces of calendar-freedom]
\label{thm:faces}
Let $\{Y_T\}$ have unit mean, $C_T(k)=\E[(Y_T-e^k)^+]$ the normalized calls,
$G_T(\alpha)=\int_\alpha^1Q_{Y,T}(u)\,\dd u$ the integrated upper-quantile
curves, and $w_T(k)$ the total implied variances. For $T_i<T_j$ the
following are equivalent:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.8em,label=(\roman*)]
\item $C_{T_i}(k)\le C_{T_j}(k)$ for all $k$ (no calendar-spread arbitrage);
\item $Y_{T_i}\le_{cx}Y_{T_j}$ (increasing convex order);
\item $G_{T_i}(\alpha)\le G_{T_j}(\alpha)$ for all $\alpha\in[0,1]$, with
equality at $\alpha=0$;
\item $w_{T_i}(k)\le w_{T_j}(k)$ for all $k$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
(i)$\Leftrightarrow$(ii): call payoffs $(y-e^k)^+$ span the convex functions
of $y$ (every convex function with equal means integrates against the second
derivative of the call in strike --- the Breeden--Litzenberger pairing), so
ordering all calls with equal means is exactly convex order
\cite{Strassen1965,CarrMadan2005}.
(ii)$\Leftrightarrow$(iii): the Hardy--Littlewood characterization ---
$\E\phi(Y_i)\le\E\phi(Y_j)$ for all convex $\phi$ iff the integrated
quantile functions are ordered; $G$ is the upper-tail version, and
$G(0)=\E Y=1$ pins the means.
(i)$\Leftrightarrow$(iv): the normalized Black price is strictly increasing
in total variance at fixed $k$, so ordering prices at every $k$ is ordering
the implied $w$ at every $k$ \cite{GatheralJacquier2014}.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[Kellerer: why this is the whole story]
\label{rem:kellerer}
If in addition each slice is butterfly-free (a valid density --- Notes
01--04), then (ii) for all pairs makes the family
$\{\mathrm{law}(Y_T)\}$ a \emph{peacock}, and Kellerer's theorem supplies a
martingale with exactly these marginals \cite{Kellerer1972}. So
butterfly-freedom per slice plus calendar-freedom across slices is not
merely ``no static arbitrage'': it is precisely the statement that the
fitted surface is consistent with \emph{some} arbitrage-free dynamic model,
even though the fitter never names one.
\end{remark}

\begin{heuristic}
Convex order with equal means says the longer law is a mean-preserving
spread of the shorter: same forward, more dispersion --- exactly what a
diffusion does as variance accumulates. Enforcing it between
independently-fitted expiries is what stops two good slice fits from
implying a negative forward variance between them.
\end{heuristic}

\section{The LQD-exact enforcement}
\label{sec:lqd}

\begin{proposition}[The asset share is the integrated quantile curve]
\label{prop:GisA}
For an LQD slice with quantile $Q(z)$ in the logit coordinate
(Note~01), the integrated upper-quantile curve equals the asset-share
integral at the corresponding grid point:
\[
G(\alpha)
=\int_\alpha^1 e^{Q(u)}\,\dd u
=\int_{z_\alpha}^{\infty}e^{Q(z)}\,\Lambda(z)\big(1-\Lambda(z)\big)\,\dd z
=A(z_\alpha).
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Substitute $u=\Lambda(z)$, $\dd u=\Lambda(z)(1-\Lambda(z))\,\dd z$ in the
definition of $G$ and compare with Note~01's asset-share integral
$A(z)$ --- they are the same object.
\end{proof}

So \cref{thm:faces}(iii) needs no new computation: every built slice already
carries $A(z)$ on the shared quadrature grid, and the calendar constraint is
an elementwise comparison of two arrays --- no quantile inversion, no
per-strike call comparison --- with constraint points automatically dense in
the wings (the grid is uniform in $z$, not $k$).

The surface is fitted nearest-to-farthest. The constraint subgrid takes
every \calstride th node ($\sim320$ points on the $8001$-node grid), and
each new slice carries the soft squared-hinge slack
\begin{equation}
\sqrt{W_{\text{cal}}}\,\max\!\big(A_i(z_m)-A_j(z_m),\,0\big),
\qquad W_{\text{cal}}=\texttt{calendarWeight}=10^6,
\label{eq:slack}
\end{equation}
keyed on the constraint $z$-\emph{coordinates}, so a slice calibrated on a
coarser optimization grid enforces it by Hermite evaluation, bit-for-bit
equivalent to the node-indexed form on the native grid.

\section{The model-agnostic enforcement}
\label{sec:agnostic}

The SVI and Multi-Core Sigmoid (MCS) overlays carry no quantile curve, so
they use face (iv) directly: a soft hinge on total variance,
\begin{equation}
\sqrt{W_{\text{cal}}}\,\max\!\big(w_{\text{near}}(k_m)-w_{\text{far}}(k_m),\,0\big),
\label{eq:wfloor}
\end{equation}
on a log-moneyness grid, reading only the previous slice's
$\texttt{implied\_w}(k)$. (The local-vol surface needs \emph{neither}
enforcement: a positive local variance is calendar-clean by construction ---
Note~04's \cref{thm:faces}(iv) holds along the whole PDE march --- so the
floor threads only to the parametric overlays.)

The production plumbing is the part that took care. The floor requires the
\emph{previous} displayed slice, so \texttt{enforceCalendar} threads
\texttt{prev\_display} ascending-$T$ through every path that fits a slice:
the single-slice overlay, \texttt{fit\_and\_commit\_slice}, the full-surface
loop, and the coupled Calibrate workflow all pass the just-fitted display to
the next expiry. Wherever the floor is absent (\texttt{off}, or the first
expiry) the residual block is absent too --- byte-identical, test-locked for
SVI, MCS, and the display pipeline.

\section{Case file: the phantom calendar}
\label{sec:phantom}

\begin{example}[title={Case file: the phantom calendar violation}]
\textbf{Setup.} A steep short-dated slice over a flatter long-dated one ---
a single-name skew: near SVI $b=0.20,\rho=-0.7$; far $b=0.08,\rho=-0.3$;
the far expiry's quotes span only $k\in[-0.25,0.20]$. On the data, the far
slice sits \emph{above} the near one everywhere: genuinely
calendar-consistent.

\textbf{Failure mode.} Live SVI overlays on NVDA (Sep-26) and SPY (Jun-27)
fitted \emph{flat} with huge RMS whenever the calendar toggle was on.

\textbf{Diagnosis.} The floor \eqref{eq:wfloor} was sampled on a fixed wide
grid $k\in[-1,1]$. With \emph{linear} wings, the steep near slice
extrapolates to far higher variance at the grid edges than the flat far
slice --- so $w_{\text{near}}>w_{\text{far}}$ out where neither expiry has a
single quote. A \emph{phantom} violation, manufactured entirely by two
extrapolation laws disagreeing in a no-data region; the hinge then flattened
the far fit to cure a problem the data never posed
(\cref{fig:phantom}, left).

\textbf{Fix.} Confinement: \texttt{variance\_floor\_grid\_from} builds the
floor grid on the observed quote span $[\min k,\max k]$ only ($41$ points;
the wide $[-1,1]$/$161$-point grid survives only as the empty-quotes
fallback). The two-curve constraint is sampled only where both curves are
pinned by data --- the confinement principle of Note~09, of which this
incident is the founding instance.

\textbf{Verdict (reproduced with the production calibrator,
\cref{fig:phantom}).} Under the wide grid the far fit misses its own quotes
by \calphantomwide\ vol bp; under the confined grid by \calphantomdata\ ---
indistinguishable from the unconstrained fit. Test-locked, including the
assertion that the scenario is consistent on data and inconsistent only in
extrapolation.
\end{example}

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={The confinement fix,
\texttt{variance\_floor\_grid\_from} (verified in \cref{app:ref}).},
label={lst:confine}]
def variance_floor_grid_from(k_quotes, n=41):
    k = np.asarray(k_quotes, float)     # floor on the TRADED span only
    if k.size == 0:
        return variance_floor_grid()    # no data at all: wide fallback
    return np.linspace(float(k.min()), float(k.max()), n)
\end{lstlisting}

\begin{remark}[Open problem: what the extrapolated region still deserves]
\label{rem:openwings}
Confinement is kept --- it is the production rule, and the case file above is
why. It is not the last word. The confined floor enforces \emph{nothing}
outside the traded span, yet the extrapolated region is not uniformly
worthless: just past the last quote the options still carry genuine premium,
and a calendar inversion there between two \emph{stated} wing contracts
(Note~09) is a real inconsistency even if no quote witnesses it. The
candidate refinement is a \emph{softer} floor in the near-extrapolation
region --- the hinge weight decaying with distance past the last quote, or
with the options' remaining time value, so that a gross inversion where
options are demonstrably not worthless is still leaned on while the deep-wing
disagreement of two extrapolation fictions stays harmless. This is open
(flagged 2026-07): any such scheme must be designed jointly with Note~09's
per-model wing contracts and the floor-grid construction of
\cref{lst:confine}, and must preserve the case-file property that the
NVDA/SPY phantom stays cured.
\end{remark}

\section{Worked example: curing a real violation}
\label{sec:example}

Confinement removes \emph{phantom} violations; this example shows the floor
doing its real job. \Cref{fig:cross} fits an \emph{inverted-wing} pair: a
high-vol short expiry ($T=0.25$) over a calmer long one ($T=0.5$), the term
structure an imminent event produces. Fitted independently, the long slice's
total variance dips below the short one's in the wings --- a genuine
calendar violation of \calviolfree. Refitting under the calendar floor lifts
it to dominate everywhere ($\calviolcured$, numerically zero), and the
integrated upper-quantile curves come out correctly ordered,
$G_{\text{far}}\ge G_{\text{near}}$ --- \cref{thm:faces}(iii) made visible.

\section{What is original here}
\label{sec:original}

The convex-order characterization is classical; the contributions are
implementation. Recognizing that the LQD asset share \emph{is} the
integrated upper-quantile curve (\cref{prop:GisA}) makes the calendar
constraint a free elementwise comparison rather than a grid of inverted
call inequalities. Extending one calendar treatment to every parametric
overlay through the total-variance floor \eqref{eq:wfloor} --- threaded
ascending-$T$ through every calibration path --- gives the surface a uniform
guarantee regardless of which model draws each slice. And the phantom
calendar of \cref{sec:phantom} is the incident that crystallized the
confinement principle now applied in three other places (Notes 04, 05, 09).

\section{Reference implementation}
\label{app:ref}

The two enforcement residuals are one line each, and the confined grid is
\cref{lst:confine}. The hinge below reproduces the residual blocks inside
the SVI/MCS calibrators exactly (the production rows are inline in each
calibrator, not a shared function --- deliberately, since each model
evaluates its own $w$); \cref{lst:confine} matches
\texttt{variance\_floor\_grid\_from} output to machine precision on quoted
and empty inputs alike.

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={The calendar hinge as it appears (inline) in
each parametric calibrator, and the LQD asset-share form.}]
# SVI / Multi-Core Sigmoid (face (iv)): rows are zero unless the far dips
cal = np.sqrt(calendar_weight) * np.maximum(cal_floor - w_model, 0.0)

# LQD (face (iii)): the same hinge on the asset share A(z)
cal = np.sqrt(cal_weight) * np.maximum(cal_floor - slice_.asset_share_at(cal_z), 0.0)
\end{lstlisting}
