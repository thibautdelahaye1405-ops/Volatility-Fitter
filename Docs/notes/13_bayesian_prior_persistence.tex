\begin{abstract}
A desk does not re-derive its volatility surface from scratch each morning: it
\emph{persists} yesterday's surface and lets today's quotes update it. Done
naively this is a dilemma --- anchor hard and you \emph{damp} a genuine
overnight move; anchor softly and the unquoted wings \emph{flap}. This note
documents the Vol-Fitter's resolution: gated Bayesian persistence, where every
mode adds residual rows of one universal shape and an \emph{activation gate}
turns each row off exactly where today's quotes already identify its feature.
We give formal definitions of the two gating mechanisms actually shipped ---
the precision-ratio gate of the operator and factor modes, and the
coverage-deficit weighting of the strike-gap mode --- and prove the three
statements the design rests on: a closed gate contributes nothing to the
objective (the no-damp guarantee), the propagated precision of a signed basket
is the weighted harmonic support of its legs, and persisting RR/BF as
\emph{baskets} rather than legs preserves exactly the level direction that
per-leg anchoring would damp. The seven persistence modes, the diagnostics
endpoint, and the transported-prior provenance are specified against the code.
Two case files close the loop: the 4-vol-point overnight jump where the
operator prior tracks the jumped wing while the strike anchor clings to
yesterday's level, and the temporal backtest that \emph{confirmed} the shipped
hybrid default ($\sim32$ vol bps better held-out wing reconstruction than no
prior, two-thirds of the time) while honestly convicting the pure operator
modes of adding nothing at the median. The worked example, built with the
production calibrator, shows an ATM jump preserved (\priatmpersist\% vs the
market's \priatmmarket\%) while the unquoted wing is held
(\priwingps\% vs the market-only fit's \priwingmo\%).
\end{abstract}

\section{The morning dilemma}
\label{sec:dilemma}

Overnight, two things change unevenly. The at-the-money level may move sharply
on fresh news, and it is \emph{well quoted} --- the liquid near-the-money
options re-price immediately. The wings may have \emph{no} fresh quotes at
all, yet they still need values. A fitter faces a choice: re-fit from today's
quotes only, and the model extrapolates the (now steeper) ATM skew into
implausible tails --- the wings \emph{flap}; or anchor hard to yesterday's
prior, and the prior pulls the \emph{ATM} back toward yesterday --- the real
signal is \emph{damped}.

\begin{invariant}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{Do not damp the signal.} A feature today's quotes identify is
never pulled toward the prior: its gate is exactly zero, not merely small.
\item \textbf{Fill the gap.} A feature today's quotes do not identify is held
at the (transported) prior, with strength that grows as coverage falls.
\item \textbf{Strictly additive.} Mode \texttt{off} is byte-identical to a
plain fit; every prior block is a no-op when its target is \texttt{None}.
\item \textbf{Coupled features persist coupled.} A risk-reversal is one
statement, not two leg statements; persistence must not silently decompose it.
\end{enumerate}
\end{invariant}

\begin{heuristic}
The resolution is to make the prior's strength \emph{local} and
\emph{data-aware}: strong in the wings (no quotes $\Rightarrow$ trust
yesterday), zero at the money (fresh quotes $\Rightarrow$ trust today). This
is ordinary Bayesian updating with an observation-dependent prior precision
--- stated as an operational rule: \emph{do not damp the signal}.
\end{heuristic}

\section{The universal residual and the gate}
\label{sec:gate}

Every persistence mode adds least-squares rows of one shape: a functional of
the model minus the same functional of the transported prior, carried at a
gated weight,
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;r_j=\sqrt{\lambda_j}\;
\frac{O_j(\text{model})-O_j(\text{prior})}{\text{scale}_j},\;}
\label{eq:universal}
\end{equation}
where $O_j$ is a call price at a delta location, a quote operator
(ATM/RR/BF/var-swap), or a local smile factor, depending on the mode
(\cref{sec:modes}).

\begin{definition}[Activation gate]
\label{def:gate}
For an observation precision $\pi^{\text{obs}}\ge0$, a required precision
$\pi^{\text{req}}>0$ and a sharpness $\gamma>0$,
\begin{equation}
\mathrm{gap}\big(\pi^{\text{obs}}\big)
=\Big(\min\big(\max\big(1-\pi^{\text{obs}}/\pi^{\text{req}},\,0\big),\,1\big)\Big)^{\gamma}.
\label{eq:gap}
\end{equation}
\end{definition}

The gate is $1$ where the data says nothing, decreases monotonically as the
data improves, and --- the decisive property --- is \emph{exactly zero} for
all $\pi^{\text{obs}}\ge\pi^{\text{req}}$, not asymptotically small.
$\gamma$ sharpens the edge (\cref{fig:gate}).

\begin{proposition}[The no-damp guarantee]
\label{prop:nodamp}
Fix a mode and let coordinate $j$ satisfy
$\pi^{\text{obs}}_j\ge\pi^{\text{req}}_j$, so $\mathrm{gap}_j=0$ and hence
$\lambda_j=0$ in \eqref{eq:universal}. Then the row $r_j\equiv0$ for
\emph{every} model parameter vector: the objective, its gradient and its
Gauss--Newton model are independent of the prior value $O_j(\text{prior})$.
In particular, with all gates closed the fit is the prior-free fit, and with
mode \texttt{off} the fit is byte-identical to a plain fit (test-locked).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
$\lambda_j=0$ makes $r_j$ the zero function of $\theta$, so it contributes
$0$ to the cost and a zero row to the Jacobian; nothing downstream can depend
on the prior through it. The byte-identity claims are locked by
\texttt{test\_none\_anchor\_leaves\_the\_fit\_byte\_identical} and the
\texttt{off}-mode routing tests.
\end{proof}

This is the difference between gating and shrinkage: a ridge toward the prior
with a small weight still biases every coordinate a little; the gate biases
identified coordinates \emph{not at all}, and spends its entire budget on the
unidentified ones.

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={The activation gate \eqref{eq:gap}, verbatim from
the shared vocabulary (\texttt{calib/precision.py}); matches production
exactly (\cref{app:ref}).},label={lst:gate}]
def activation_gap(obs, req, gamma=1.0):  # 1: data silent; 0: identified
    return np.clip(1 - obs/np.maximum(req, 1e-12), 0.0, 1.0)**gamma
\end{lstlisting}

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.72\textwidth]{fig_prior_gate.pdf}
\caption{The activation gate \eqref{eq:gap}. Past the required precision the
prior weight is exactly zero --- the ``do not damp the signal'' rule.
$\gamma$ controls how hard the on/off edge is.}
\label{fig:gate}
\end{figure}

\section{Two gates, honestly distinguished}
\label{sec:twogates}

Production ships \emph{two} gating mechanisms, and earlier revisions of this
note blurred them. They answer the same question --- \emph{where should the
prior pull?} --- with different mathematics.

\subsection{The operator/factor gate: a precision ratio}
\label{sec:opgate}

For the operator and factor modes, the observation precision of a coordinate
is its \emph{quote support}: each leg location $k_a$ receives the
Gaussian-kernel effective quote count
$s_a=\sum_q \omega_q\,e^{-(k_q-k_a)^2/2b^2}$ with bandwidth
$b=\texttt{priorOperatorBandwidth}$, and a basket
$O=\sum_a c_a\,\sigma(k_a)$ aggregates its legs' support harmonically:
\begin{equation}
\pi^{\text{obs}}(O)=\Big(\sum_a\frac{c_a^2}{s_a+\eps}\Big)^{-1}.
\label{eq:harmonic}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[Harmonic support is the propagated basket precision]
\label{prop:harmonic}
Suppose each leg vol is estimable from today's quotes with variance
proportional to $1/s_a$, independently across legs. Then the implied estimate
of the basket $O=\sum_ac_a\sigma(k_a)$ has variance proportional to
$\sum_ac_a^2/s_a$, i.e.\ precision proportional to \eqref{eq:harmonic}. In
particular one unquoted leg ($s_a\to0$) drives $\pi^{\text{obs}}(O)\to0$
however well the other leg is quoted: a risk-reversal with a missing put leg
is \emph{unidentified}, and its gate opens.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
$\Var\big(\sum_ac_a\hat\sigma_a\big)=\sum_ac_a^2\Var(\hat\sigma_a)
\propto\sum_ac_a^2/s_a$ by independence; take reciprocals.
\end{proof}

The active weights split a budget $B$ over the open gates,
\begin{equation}
\lambda_j=B\,\frac{\mathrm{gap}_j}{\sum_i \mathrm{gap}_i},
\qquad B=\frac{\texttt{priorOperatorStrengthPct}}{100}\sum_q\omega_q ,
\label{eq:budget}
\end{equation}
and operators with $\mathrm{gap}_j=0$ are dropped from the target entirely.

\begin{remark}[What the shipped gate does \emph{not} use]
\label{rem:notused}
The required precision $\pi^{\text{req}}$ is the single scalar
\texttt{priorOperatorRequiredPrecision} for all operators (a per-operator
schedule is declared in the design but not implemented), and the budget split
\eqref{eq:budget} does \emph{not} multiply a base prior precision. The richer
vocabulary of \cref{sec:vocab} --- spread, freshness, transport-distance and
fit-quality factors, and \texttt{active\_prior\_precision} --- is consumed by
the \emph{graph baseline} of Note~14, not by this gate.
\end{remark}

\subsection{The strike-gap weighting: a coverage deficit}
\label{sec:sggate}

The strike-gap mode gates by a different object: a \emph{density deficit} in
log-moneyness. With $\rho_{\text{obs}}$ the Gaussian KDE of today's quote
locations (total mass $=$ quote count, bandwidth $0.06$) and
$\rho_{\text{des}}$ a desired coverage of the same mass spread over the wider
anchor span, each anchor strike $x_j$ (placed at the deltas
$\{2,5,10,25,40\}\Delta$ by the Black delta map
$k=\tfrac12\sigma^2\tau-\sigma\sqrt\tau\,\Phi^{-1}(c)$) receives
\begin{equation}
\text{weight}_j
=B_{\text{sg}}\cdot
\frac{\big(\rho_{\text{des}}(x_j)-\rho_{\text{obs}}(x_j)\big)^{+}\,\Delta x_j}
{\sum_i\big(\rho_{\text{des}}(x_i)-\rho_{\text{obs}}(x_i)\big)^{+}\,\Delta x_i},
\qquad
\text{unmet}=\frac{\sum_i(\cdots)^+\Delta x_i}{\text{desired mass}},
\label{eq:deficit}
\end{equation}
with $\Delta x_j$ the anchor's Voronoi cell width, and the residual is the
vega-normalized \emph{call-price} difference at $x_j$ (the deep-wing
$1/\text{vega}$ capped at $25\times$ its minimum so an anchor cannot amplify
noise unboundedly). Where quotes already meet the desired density the deficit
is zero --- the same ``off where the data speaks'' behaviour as
\cref{def:gate}, but expressed as coverage in strike space rather than a
precision ratio, and never multiplied by a prior precision. The companion
var-swap pull is scaled by the \emph{unmet} fraction: a fully covered smile
persists nothing, including the var-swap level.

\begin{caution}
The two mechanisms persist different \emph{things}. Strike-gap rows anchor
\emph{absolute call prices}: if the whole vol level jumps overnight, an
unquoted wing is pulled toward yesterday's \emph{absolute} wing --- correct
when the level is stable, wrong across a jump. Operator/factor rows anchor
\emph{level-invariant shape} (RR, BF, curvature are unchanged by
$\sigma\mapsto\sigma+c$), so the persisted wing rides \emph{today's} level.
The first case file of \cref{sec:cases} measures exactly this distinction ---
it is why the hybrid default carries the shape on operators and reserves
strike anchors for the deep tail no operator covers.
\end{caution}

\subsection{The shared vocabulary and its consumers}
\label{sec:vocab}

\texttt{calib/precision.py} is the single home of the gate
\eqref{eq:gap} and the scalar confidence factors
\begin{equation}
\begin{aligned}
\text{density}&=\mathrm{clip}(n/8,\,0.15,\,1), &
\text{spread}&=\frac{1}{1+\text{rel spread}/0.05},\\
\text{freshness}&=2^{-\text{age}/\text{half-life}}\ (3\,\text{d obs},\ 30\,\text{d prior}), &
\text{transport}&=e^{-|h|/0.10},
\end{aligned}
\label{eq:factors}
\end{equation}
with $|h|=|\log(F_{\text{now}}/F_{\text{prior}})|$ the transported forward
distance. The persistence gate of \cref{sec:opgate} consumes only the quote
support; the \emph{full} vocabulary --- fit quality $1/\text{rms}^2$ floored
at 1 vol bp, provenance tiers, and the factors \eqref{eq:factors} --- prices
the graph baseline of Note~14, which imports these functions from here
(re-export golden-locked, so the two systems cannot drift apart).

\section{Operators, factors, and why baskets}
\label{sec:operators}

\begin{definition}[Quote operators]
\label{def:operators}
With $\sigma(k)$ a slice's implied vol, $k_{c,\delta},k_{p,\delta}$ the
call/put strikes at delta $\delta$ on the \emph{prior} smile, and
\texttt{collarSign} fixing the RR orientation:
\[
\text{ATM}=\sigma(0),\qquad
\text{RR}_\delta=\pm\big(\sigma(k_{c,\delta})-\sigma(k_{p,\delta})\big),\qquad
\text{BF}_\delta=\tfrac12\big(\sigma(k_{c,\delta})+\sigma(k_{p,\delta})\big)-\sigma(0),
\]
plus $\text{VarSwap}=\sqrt{K_{\text{var}}/\tau}$. Each is a \emph{signed
basket} $O=\sum_ac_a\sigma(k_a)$ over frozen leg locations; the known set is
$\{$ATM, RR25, BF25, RR10, BF10, VarSwap$\}$.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Smile factors]
\label{def:factors}
The factor mode replaces delta legs by ATM-local stencils of step
$h=\texttt{priorOperatorBandwidth}$:
$\text{skew}=\sigma(h)-\sigma(-h)$,
$\text{curvature}=\sigma(h)-2\sigma(0)+\sigma(-h)$,
$\text{leftWing}=\sigma(-3h)-\sigma(0)$,
$\text{rightWing}=\sigma(3h)-\sigma(0)$ (raw differences, not divided by
$h$) --- the prior's \emph{shape} re-expressed at the current node. Factors
reuse the operator gate and target machinery verbatim.
\end{definition}

\begin{proposition}[Baskets preserve exactly the directions legs would damp]
\label{prop:nullspace}
Consider one operator $O=\sum_ac_a\sigma_a$ over $L$ legs, and compare two
penalties on the leg vols $\sigma\in\R^L$:
the basket row
$r_{\text{bsk}}=\sqrt\lambda\,(c\T\sigma-c\T\sigma^{\text{prior}})$ and the
per-leg rows
$r_a=\sqrt{\lambda_a}\,(\sigma_a-\sigma_a^{\text{prior}})$, $a=1..L$. The
basket penalty is invariant under every leg move $\delta$ with
$c\T\delta=0$ --- an $(L{-}1)$-dimensional space that, for RR and BF,
contains the \emph{common level shift} $\delta=\1$ (since the coefficients
sum to zero). The per-leg penalty is invariant only under $\delta=0$: it
penalizes the level shift with weight $\sum_a\lambda_a$, i.e.\ it damps a
market-wide vol move even when the market's RR and BF are unchanged.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
$r_{\text{bsk}}$ depends on $\sigma$ only through $c\T\sigma$, so its level
sets are affine hyperplanes with normal $c$; adding any
$\delta\in c^\perp$ leaves it fixed, and $\dim c^\perp=L-1$. For RR,
$c=(+1,-1)$; for BF, $c=(\tfrac12,\tfrac12,-1)$; both satisfy $c\T\1=0$, so
$\1\in c^\perp$. The per-leg stack has Jacobian
$\diag(\sqrt{\lambda_a})$ of full rank, so its only invariant direction is
$0$, and its value at $\sigma^{\text{prior}}+t\1$ grows like
$t^2\sum_a\lambda_a$.
\end{proof}

This is invariant~4 made precise, and it is why the LV route was
\emph{changed} during the build: the first design emitted synthetic per-leg
quotes, which silently damped the level; the shipped design emits one signed
\texttt{BasketQuote} per operator. On the local-vol surface, where the
natural unknowns are call prices, the basket is transported to price space by
the frozen-vega linearization
$\sigma(k_a)\approx\sigma^{\text{prior}}_a+(P_a-P^{\text{prior}}_a)/\text{vega}_a$:
weights $w_a=c_a/\text{vega}_a$, target $\sum_aw_aP^{\text{prior}}_a$,
tolerance $1/\sqrt\lambda$. It also cured a long-standing asymmetry: the SVI
and Multi-Core Sigmoid (MCS) overlays, which previously received no prior at
all, now consume the same operator targets as LQD.

percentage of the summed quote weights, so prior strength scales with how
much data the fit actually has.

\subsection{Two-pass activation}
\label{sec:twopass}

The sharpest observation precision is the \emph{realized} fit quality --- a
chicken-and-egg with the fit it modifies. The opt-in
\texttt{priorDataOnlyPrepass} resolves it exactly: fit once on data alone,
measure each operator's realized precision, refit with priors active only on
the under-observed coordinates. It is the most faithful no-damp behaviour at
$\sim2\times$ cost per node; the default single pass gates on quote support
\eqref{eq:harmonic} with no extra fit.

\section{Transport and provenance}
\label{sec:transport}

The prior is never consumed raw. Its LQD backbone (the canonical stored
object per expiry) is \emph{transported} to the current forward under the
active spot-dynamics regime (Note~12), with
$h=\log(F_{\text{now}}/F_{\text{prior}})$; all operator, factor and anchor
targets are evaluated on the transported smile $w(k)$. A snapshot stores, per
expiry, the LQD backbone plus the displayed-model parameters, forwards,
discounts and both clocks $(\tau,t)$; persistence is to the \texttt{VolStore}
database with history, and the active prior carries a provenance source
(\texttt{saved} / \texttt{15min} / \texttt{close} / \texttt{none}) and a
per-ticker monotone version that busts the fit cache --- a fetched prior can
never silently coexist with fits made under the old one.

\section{Worked example: an overnight jump}
\label{sec:example}

\Cref{fig:nodamp} is the dilemma made concrete, built with the production
strike-gap prior and calibrator. Yesterday's prior is a calm
$\priatmprior\%$-ATM smile. Overnight the market jumps: today's ATM is
$\priatmmarket\%$ with a steeper skew, and \emph{only} the ATM region
($|k|\le0.10$) is quoted. The market-only fit captures the ATM but
extrapolates the steep skew into a wild put wing ($\priwingmo\%$ at
$k=-0.30$). The prior-persisted fit keeps the ATM at the \emph{market} level
(\priatmpersist\%) because the coverage deficit \eqref{eq:deficit} is zero
where quotes are dense, while holding the unquoted wing at the prior
($\priwingps\%$ vs the prior's $\priwingprior\%$) because the deficit is
total where there are none. The signal is preserved; the gap is filled.

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.82\textwidth]{fig_prior_nodamp.pdf}
\caption{``Don't damp the signal.'' The persisted fit (solid) tracks the
market ATM jump (quoted region shaded) while the unquoted wings are held by
the prior (dotted), rather than flapping like the market-only fit (dashed).}
\label{fig:nodamp}
\end{figure}

Note what this example does \emph{and does not} show: the strike-gap anchor
holds the wing at yesterday's \emph{absolute} level, which is right here
because the wing genuinely had no information --- but across a level jump
that same absoluteness becomes the failure mode the next section measures.

\section{Case files}
\label{sec:cases}

\begin{example}[title={Case file: the jump the strike anchor damped}]
\textbf{Setup.} The scenario of \texttt{test\_prior\_nodamp.py}: a
$\tau=0.5$ node, an overnight ATM jump of $4$ vol points with the whole
smile lifted, today's quotes dense at the money and absent in the wings.
Operator prior (\texttt{ATM/RR25/BF25}) vs the legacy strike-gap anchor, both
through the production calibrator.

\textbf{Failure mode.} The strike anchor pins the unquoted wing to
yesterday's \emph{absolute} wing vol --- which after a level jump is the
\emph{wrong} wing: it under-shoots today's true (lifted) wing and drags the
nearby smile with it.

\textbf{Diagnosis.} \Cref{prop:nullspace}: per-strike price anchors penalize
the common level direction $\1$; RR/BF baskets are invariant under it. The
operator target also drops ATM automatically
(\texttt{"ATM" not in op.names}) because its quote support exceeds the
requirement --- \cref{prop:nodamp} in action.

\textbf{Fix.} Persist \emph{shape}, not level: the operator rows carry
yesterday's RR/BF onto today's level, and the wing is reconstructed as
``today's ATM $+$ yesterday's skew/convexity''.

\textbf{Verdict (test-locked).} The operator-prior wing lands closer to the
\emph{true jumped} wing than the strike anchor's, while the strike anchor's
wing stays closer to \emph{yesterday's} wing; the operator and factor fits
match the data-only ATM to $<0.4$ and $<0.6$ vol points respectively --- no
damping. The hybrid default is precisely this division of labour: operators
for shape, strike anchors only for the deep tail no operator covers.
\end{example}

\begin{example}[title={Case file: the backtest that confirmed the default}]
\textbf{Setup.} The temporal harness: for each $(\text{asset},T{-}1\to T)$
pair, fit day $T{-}1$'s full chain, freeze it as the active prior, thin day
$T$ to its ATM region, refit under each mode, and score the reconstructed
held-out moderate wing against day $T$'s true quotes; \texttt{off} is the
baseline. Full spike regime, $1117$ nodes, plus a bandwidth and var-swap
probe sweep.

\textbf{Verdict.} The shipped \textbf{hybrid} default reconstructs the
held-out wing $\sim32$ vol bps better than no prior, $\sim66\%$ of the time,
and wins at \emph{every} bandwidth and probe tested; strike-gap is a close
second. The \emph{pure} signed-operator modes do not beat \texttt{off} at
the median --- with the wing entirely dark, the reconstruction is driven by
the tail/strike anchor, not the RR/BF rows.

\textbf{Lesson.} The backtest \emph{confirmed} rather than changed the
configuration: bandwidth is not a productive lever (left at $0.06$), the
var-swap probe stays at $1.4\sigma$, and the hybrid's two components are
both necessary --- operators to avoid damping (previous case file), the tail
anchor to actually rebuild a fully dark wing. Neither alone suffices; the
composite does.
\end{example}

\section{What is original here}
\label{sec:original}

The Bayesian framing is standard; the contributions are operational. The
\emph{activation gate} \eqref{eq:gap} turns ``do not damp the signal'' into
one differentiable factor with a provable dead zone (\cref{prop:nodamp}).
The \emph{harmonic basket support} \eqref{eq:harmonic} prices identifiability
of a combination, not of legs (\cref{prop:harmonic}). The
\emph{signed-basket} operators preserve exactly the level direction per-leg
persistence would damp (\cref{prop:nullspace}) --- the theorem behind the
measured case file --- and incidentally cured the SVI/MCS missing-prior
asymmetry. The \emph{shared vocabulary} keeps the persistence gate and the
graph baseline speaking one language without letting them silently diverge
(golden re-export tests). And the whole framework is strictly additive:
\texttt{off} is byte-identical to a plain fit.

\section{Reference implementation: the gated posterior}
\label{app:ref}

The gate feeds a precision-weighted Gaussian update. For one coordinate with
prior mean $\mu_0$ at gated precision $\lambda_0$ and a datum $d$ at
precision $\lambda_d$, the posterior mean is the precision-weighted average,
which the least-squares stack realizes as the row
$\sqrt{\lambda_0}(\theta-\mu_0)$ appended to the data rows. The listing
reproduces the production \texttt{activation\_gap} exactly and states the
posterior it implies: $\lambda_0\to0$ (closed gate) gives the data,
$\lambda_d\to0$ (no data) gives the prior.

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={The gate (verbatim vs \texttt{calib/precision.py})
and the scalar posterior a gated row implements.}]
def activation_gap(obs, req, gamma=1.0):
    return np.clip(1 - obs/np.maximum(req, 1e-12), 0.0, 1.0)**gamma

def posterior(mu0, lam0, d, lam_d):    # lam0 = gated prior precision
    return (lam0*mu0 + lam_d*d) / (lam0 + lam_d)
    # lam0 -> 0: posterior -> d (no damping); lam_d -> 0: -> mu0 (gap filled)
\end{lstlisting}
