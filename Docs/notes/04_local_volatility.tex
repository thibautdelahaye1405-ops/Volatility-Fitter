\begin{abstract}
This note documents the Vol-Fitter's full local-volatility model: a continuous
piecewise-affine ($P_1$) local-variance surface $\nu_\theta(\tau,x)$,
calibrated directly to sparse vanilla and variance-swap quotes through the
forward Dupire PDE as an implicit pricing map. Positivity of the nodal
variances guarantees a positive surface on the grid's hull; the implicit-Euler
step matrix is an $M$-matrix, giving a discrete maximum principle and
unconditional $\ell^\infty$ stability (Crank--Nicolson provably loses
monotonicity on coarse grids); and butterfly/calendar cleanliness of the
output is \emph{measured} on the price grid, never assumed. Sensitivities are
an analytic tangent Jacobian assembled from forward PDE sensitivities; the
matrix-free Gauss--Newton solver avoids the dense SVD but consumes that same
Jacobian (the true backward-PDE adjoint derived here remains future theory).
Engineering --- a compiled vectorized-Thomas march, a stall early-stop, warm
seeds --- cut the cold fit $\sim3$--$6\times$. Two case files: the six-day
weekly that broke the strike grid ($108\to23.5$ vol bp) and the convex-wing
hinge that flattened SPY until confined to the extrapolation tail
($25.7\to2.6$). Fresh evidence (commit \texttt{\lvgencommit}, \lvgendate):
the synthetic quotes reprice to \lvrtmaxerr\ vol bp with the local-vol
surface itself recovered to \lvrtsurfmax\ vol points on the quote-covered
region; the product-path Bloomberg fit reads \lvspyrms\ (SPY) and
\lvnvdarms\ (NVDA) vol bp in-operator --- and \lvspyconv\ / \lvnvdaconv\ on
the \emph{converged} reprice, the honest metric this revision adopts.
\end{abstract}

\section{The production problem}
\label{sec:problem}

A slice model fits one expiry at a time and stitches expiries together with a
calendar constraint. Production needs the opposite object too: one surface that
prices \emph{every} strike and expiry of a name simultaneously --- for
scenario transport (Note~12), variance swaps (Note~08), and as the projection
target of the graph extrapolator (Note~14). The classical route --- fit an
implied surface, then \emph{differentiate} it through Dupire's formula --- is
numerically explosive exactly where production data lives: sparse wings, short
tenors, stale quotes. Dividing two noisy numerical derivatives manufactures
negative variances and spikes that no downstream PDE survives.

\begin{invariant}
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item Every optimizer iterate is a genuine, strictly positive local-variance
surface \emph{on the grid's hull} --- positivity is carried by the
\emph{parameters}, never policed by a penalty. (The controlled-linear put
extrapolation \emph{beyond} the hull is outside this guarantee;
\cref{sec:p1}.)
\item The pricing map never differentiates market data: quotes enter only as
targets of a forward PDE solve.
\item The discrete prices obey a maximum principle --- the scheme is monotone
and unconditionally $\ell^\infty$-stable. That is stability, not an arbitrage
proof: butterfly and calendar cleanliness of the output are \emph{measured}
by explicit grid diagnostics, never assumed.
\item The short-dated rescues and speed levers are gated; each gate's no-op
behaviour is \emph{individually} demonstrated (byte-identity where locked as
such, stated tolerance where not) --- there is no blanket byte-identity
claim.
\end{enumerate}
\end{invariant}

\paragraph{The pipeline in one line.} Normalize and de-Americanize the quotes
(Notes 05--06) $\to$ build the vertex grid and PDE lattice
(\cref{sec:p1}) $\to$ march prices and sensitivities forward
(\cref{sec:pricing,sec:sens}) $\to$ form the residual stack
(\cref{sec:objective}) $\to$ regularize and optimize under box bounds
(\cref{sec:solvers}) $\to$ reprice on the \emph{refined} operator
(the converged metric) $\to$ report the convexity/calendar diagnostics.

The Vol-Fitter therefore follows the Andreasen--Huge philosophy
\cite{AndreasenHuge2011}: \emph{parametrize} the local variance directly,
price \emph{forward} through the Dupire PDE, and calibrate the parameters to
the quotes.

\begin{heuristic}
Extracting local vol by
$\sigma_{\text{loc}}^2=\partial_TC/(\tfrac12K^2\partial_{KK}C)$ divides two
noisy derivatives of a sparse surface --- garbage in the wings. Calibrating
\emph{forward} never differentiates the data: it asks ``what positive surface,
run through the PDE, best reprices the quotes?'' Positivity is imposed on the
parameters, so every iterate is arbitrage-clean by construction --- the LQD
philosophy of Note~01, one dimension up.
\end{heuristic}

\section{The forward Dupire equation}
\label{sec:dupire}

\begin{assumption}[Units and the two clocks]
\label{ass:units}
Work in forward-normalized units: $x=K/F_T$ is the normalized strike and
$c(\cdot,x)$ the normalized call, so deterministic rates, borrow and dividends
are absorbed into the forward (Note~06); the quotes are de-Americanized mids
(Note~05). Three time objects are distinct: $T$ is the \emph{calendar}
maturity; $\tau(T)$ is the monotone \emph{event-weighted variance time} of
Note~11 (the diffusion clock, equal to $T$ when the event clock is off); and
the model's nodal parameters $\theta$ are local variances \emph{per unit
variance time} --- the PDE below marches in $\tau$, and every maturity-like
symbol in this note ($\Delta T=0.01$, the SDE clock, the var-swap integral,
the time vertices) lives on the $\tau$ axis. In calendar time the same
dynamics read $\partial_Tc=\tfrac12\,\dot\tau(T)\,\nu(\tau(T),x)\,x^2\,
\partial_{xx}c$. The model is a pure diffusion: no jumps, so a genuine
short-dated event spike is only approximated.
\end{assumption}

Writing local variance $\nu(\tau,x)=\sigma_{\text{loc}}^2(\tau,x)$ on the
variance clock, the forward Dupire equation is
\begin{equation}
\boxed{\;\partial_\tau c(\tau,x)=\tfrac12\,\nu(\tau,x)\,x^2\,\partial_{xx}c(\tau,x),\qquad
c(0,x)=(1-x)^+.\;}
\label{eq:dupire}
\end{equation}
It is a forward parabolic PDE in $(\tau,x)$ with the payoff as initial data
\cite{Dupire1994}. (Below we write a generic $t$ or $T$ for the marching
variable where no confusion is possible; it always means $\tau$.) Its
structural virtue is that positivity of $\nu$ \emph{propagates} both
static-arbitrage conditions:

\begin{proposition}[Positive local variance propagates no-arbitrage]
\label{prop:propagate}
Let $c$ solve \eqref{eq:dupire} with $\nu\ge0$ bounded and the payoff initial
condition. Then for all $\tau>0$: (i) $\partial_{xx}c(\tau,\cdot)\ge0$ (no
butterfly arbitrage); (ii) $\partial_\tau c(\tau,x)\ge0$ --- monotonicity of
the \emph{normalized} call at fixed $x=K/F_T$. Under deterministic carry this
is equivalent to the familiar calendar-spread condition at fixed dollar
strike $K$: the fixed-$K$ spread maps to the fixed-$x$ one through the
forward transformation $K\mapsto x=K/F_T$ and the discounting absorbed in the
normalization (Note~06), so no separate condition is needed --- but the two
statements are related by that transformation, not identical coordinates.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}[Proof sketch]
Let $p=\partial_{xx}c$. Differentiating \eqref{eq:dupire} twice in $x$ gives
the forward (Fokker--Planck-type) equation
$\partial_Tp=\tfrac12\,\partial_{xx}\!\big(\nu x^2p\big)$, whose initial datum
is the Dirac mass of the payoff kink at $x=1$ --- nonnegative. Such equations
preserve nonnegativity (parabolic maximum principle applied to the adjoint, or
probabilistically: $p(T,\cdot)$ is the density of the diffusion
$\dd X_t=\sqrt{\nu(t,X_t)}\,X_t\,\dd W_t$ started at $1$). With $p\ge0$
established, (ii) is immediate from \eqref{eq:dupire}:
$\partial_Tc=\tfrac12\nu x^2p\ge0$.
\end{proof}

The whole design of this model is to make \cref{prop:propagate} survive
discretization (\cref{sec:pricing}) and parametrization (\cref{sec:p1}) ---
and then to \emph{measure} what little the discretization leaks
(\cref{sec:diagnostics}).

\section{The piecewise-affine surface}
\label{sec:p1}

\subsection{Parametrization}

\begin{definition}[$P_1$ local-variance surface]
\label{def:p1}
On a tensor grid of $n_t\times n_x$ vertices $(\tau_i,\xi_j)$ the local
variance is the continuous piecewise-affine interpolant
\begin{equation}
\nu_\theta(t,x)=\sum_{i,j}\theta_{ij}\,\phi_{ij}(t,x),
\label{eq:p1}
\end{equation}
where the hat functions $\phi_{ij}$ are barycentric on a (cached Delaunay)
triangulation of the vertices --- each grid rectangle is split into two
triangles, and the interpolant is genuinely affine on each --- and
$\theta_{ij}=\nu(\tau_i,\xi_j)>0$ are the \emph{nodal local variances}, the
calibration parameters. The flat vector is time-major
$\theta=\operatorname{vec}(\theta)$.
\end{definition}

(A bilinear tensor interpolant is kept for comparison, but bilinear is not
affine on cells; the Delaunay barycentric basis is the golden convention.)

\begin{proposition}[Nodal positivity is surface positivity]
\label{prop:nodal}
On each triangle a $P_1$ function is a convex combination of its three vertex
values. Hence $\theta_{ij}\ge\nu_{\mathrm{lo}}>0$ for all $i,j$ implies
$\nu_\theta(t,x)\ge\nu_{\mathrm{lo}}$ everywhere on the grid's hull, and the
basis is a partition of unity (test-locked).
\end{proposition}

This is why the calibration needs only \emph{box bounds} on $\theta$:
positivity of a two-dimensional surface reduces to positivity of finitely many
numbers. In production the box is $[\texttt{varLo},\,\nu_{\mathrm{hi}}]$ with
\texttt{varLo}$\,=0.0025$ (local vol $5\%$) and an \emph{adaptive} cap
$\nu_{\mathrm{hi}}=\min\big(\max(0.36,\;(\texttt{lvVolCapMult}\cdot
\sigma_{\max})^2\big),\,16\big)$ --- at least $60\%$ vol, three times the
largest observed implied vol if that is larger, hard-capped at $400\%$. (The
tighter $[0.005,0.20]$ box quoted in older revisions is only the model-layer
function default used by the golden tests.)

Beyond the lowest strike vertex the surface extrapolates local variance
linearly with slope \texttt{left\_extrap\_a} times the first interior cell's
slope. Three regimes: $a=0$, the hot-path default, is a flat clamp;
\texttt{convexWing=true} sets $a$ to the \emph{fixed} multiple
\texttt{leftWingSlopeMult} ($1.5$) even without a variance-swap quote; and $a$
becomes a \emph{free} calibration variable, bounded $[0,20]$, exactly when a
var-swap quote is present (Note~08). The right wing is flat-clamped.

\begin{caution}[title={Caution: the extrapolation is outside the positivity guarantee}]
\Cref{prop:nodal} stops at the triangulation hull. Outside it the basis
carries signed weights and the linear continuation is unconstrained in both
directions: with $a>0$ and a first cell whose variance \emph{decreases}
toward $x_{\min}$, the extrapolated variance can go \emph{negative} before
$x=0$ (pricing floors it, so the priced curve kinks rather than blows up);
symmetrically, a rising continuation exceeds the nodal cap freely --- the cap
is deliberately not applied in the extrapolation region
(\nolinkurl{models/localvol/affine.py}). Every guarantee in this note that
leans on $\nu\le\nu_{\mathrm{hi}}$ or $\nu>0$ is therefore scoped to the hull
(or to the flat-wing $a=0$ path, where the clamp inherits the boundary
vertex's box); an extrapolation-domain positivity constraint is an open
follow-up.
\end{caution}

\begin{remark}[Lee's bound, on the hull]
\label{rem:lee}
Bounded positive local variance bounds the total implied variance uniformly
in strike --- by comparison with the constant-variance diffusion,
$w(k,T)\le\nu_{\mathrm{hi}}\,T$ --- so the implied wing slope
$\dd w/\dd|k|$ decays to zero, strictly inside Lee's cap of $2$ (Note~09).
Scope this like everything above: the comparison holds where
$\nu\le\nu_{\mathrm{hi}}$ holds, i.e.\ on the hull and on the flat-clamped
wings; a steep free-$a$ put continuation escapes the cap and with it this
bound. Unlike the parametric slices no wing penalty is carried, and the
affine surface currently exposes no per-fit Lee-slope fields --- wing
behaviour is a stated extrapolation contract plus the grid diagnostics of
\cref{sec:diagnostics}, not a reported slope.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Grid design, and the short-dated stack}
\label{sec:griddesign}

The strike vertices are delta-spaced (at the deltas
$\{0.01,0.02,0.05,0.10,0.25,0.40,0.50\}$ and their call mirrors, sized by the
\emph{longest} expiry's ATM std) and floored per expiry so a narrow short-dated
smile still lands enough vertices on its curvature (\texttt{gridXNodes},
\texttt{gridXMinPerExpiry}; the case file of \cref{sec:cases} is exactly the
failure that motivated the floor). The time vertices (\texttt{gridTNodes})
carry the term structure. The grid is deliberately modest --- a dozen strike
vertices, ten in time --- because each vertex is a free parameter and PDE cost
scales with the grid; the roughness regularizer (\cref{sec:objective}) supplies
the smoothness a coarse grid would otherwise lack.

The short end has since accreted a full rescue \emph{stack} (the 2026-07-11
daily-ladder pass, which post-dates and extends the original case file):
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{Adaptive variance box.} The cap is
$\nu_{\mathrm{hi}}=\min(\max(0.36,(\texttt{lvVolCapMult}\cdot
\sigma_{\max})^2),16)$ as above; the \emph{floor} is now adaptive
symmetrically, $\nu_{\mathrm{lo}}=\min\big(\text{request floor},\,
(0.5\,\min_i\sigma_{\mathrm{ATM},i})^2\big)$ --- a low-vol short-dated smile
needs local vol \emph{below} its minimum implied (implied vol is a path
average), and the fixed $5\%$ floor was measured riding the box on SPY 2-DTE
upside quotes. Keyed on ATM implieds deliberately, so a noisy deep-wing quote
cannot drag the floor down where the box does real stabilization work.
\item \textbf{PDE strike refinement.} $\Delta x$ refines to
$0.15\times$ the shortest expiry's ATM $\sigma\sqrt\tau$ (was $0.3\times$),
snapped so $x=1$ stays a node, capped at $800$ nodes (was $400$): on 2-DTE
dailies the \emph{quote spacing} is finer than the lattice, and the drawn
smile wiggled at quote frequency until the step out-resolved it.
\item \textbf{PDE time refinement.} Any short maturity interval that would
receive fewer than $8$ implicit steps at the $\Delta\tau=0.01$ ceiling is
marched with $32$ steps instead (a 2-day interval otherwise gets \emph{one}).
\item \textbf{Even-gap coverage.} For expiries up to ten days the per-expiry
count floor is upgraded to an \emph{even-gap} rule --- keep splitting the
widest boundary-augmented gap until none exceeds
$\text{range}/(m_{\min}-1)$ --- because the count floor alone is side-blind:
a downside-heavy axis met it with a single upside vertex and the daily front
drew a V through its call quotes.
\item \textbf{Chained front tie.} When the first quoted expiry is below
$0.08$ years, the front tie chains over \emph{every} vertex row before it
(quotes pin only the variance integral to $T_1$, so each sub-front row is
individually unidentified) at an effective weight of at least $1$ (up from
$10^{-2}$) --- measured $5$--$30$ vol-point ringing between sub-front rows
collapses, and operator refinement becomes monotone.
\end{enumerate}
Each rescue is gated on the regime that needs it; normal surfaces take none
of these branches (locked byte-identical where a lock exists, e.g.\ the
coverage floor and $\Delta x$ gates).

\section{The discrete pricing map}
\label{sec:pricing}

\subsection{Discretization}

The forward map \eqref{eq:dupire} is solved on a uniform strike grid with step
$\Delta x=0.01$ on $x\in[0,x_{\max}]$, $x_{\max}\gtrsim2.5$ ($\sim$250 nodes;
the short-dated refinement of \cref{sec:cases} can tighten $\Delta x$ to
$1/400$), with Dirichlet boundaries $c(\cdot,0)=1$, $c(\cdot,x_{\max})=0$, by
fully implicit (backward) Euler steps of size $\Delta T=0.01$, the local
variance entering at the \emph{new} time level. On a (possibly non-uniform)
grid with gaps $h^-_i=x_i-x_{i-1}$, $h^+_i=x_{i+1}-x_i$, the operator
$A$ encoding $\tfrac12\nu x^2\partial_{xx}$ has the three-point stencil
\begin{equation}
A_{i,i\mp1}=\nu_i\,a^\mp_i,\qquad
A_{ii}=-\nu_i(a^-_i+a^+_i),\qquad
a^\mp_i=\frac{x_i^2}{(h^-_i+h^+_i)\,h^\mp_i}>0,
\label{eq:stencil}
\end{equation}
and each step solves the tridiagonal system
\begin{equation}
B^{n+1}U^{n+1}=U^{n}+(\text{boundary terms}),\qquad
B^{n+1}=I-\Delta T\,A^{n+1}.
\label{eq:implicit}
\end{equation}

\begin{proposition}[The implicit step matrix is an $M$-matrix]
\label{prop:mmatrix}
For any $\nu\ge0$ and any $\Delta T>0$, the matrix $B=I-\Delta T A$ of
\eqref{eq:implicit} satisfies: positive diagonal
$B_{ii}=1+\Delta T\nu_i(a^-_i+a^+_i)\ge1$, non-positive off-diagonals
$B_{i,i\mp1}=-\Delta T\nu_i a^\mp_i\le0$, and unit row sums at interior rows.
Hence $B$ is a strictly diagonally dominant nonsingular $M$-matrix, and
$B^{-1}\ge0$ elementwise.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
The sign pattern and row sums are read off \eqref{eq:stencil}:
$B_{ii}-|B_{i,i-1}|-|B_{i,i+1}|
=1+\Delta T\nu_i(a^-_i+a^+_i)-\Delta T\nu_i(a^-_i+a^+_i)=1>0$, so $B$ is
strictly diagonally dominant with positive diagonal and non-positive
off-diagonals --- the defining pattern of a nonsingular $M$-matrix, and
$M$-matrices are inverse-positive: $B^{-1}\ge0$ (see e.g.\ Berman--Plemmons
\cite{BermanPlemmons1994}, Theorem 6.2.3; concretely, Jacobi iteration for
$Bu=v$ with $v\ge0$ has nonnegative iterates and converges by dominance).
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[Discrete maximum principle and unconditional stability]
\label{cor:dmp}
For any $\Delta T>0$ the scheme \eqref{eq:implicit} is \emph{monotone}
($U^n\ge V^n$ with the same boundary data implies $U^{n+1}\ge V^{n+1}$) and
satisfies
$\min\big(\min_iU^n_i,\,c_{\mathrm{bdry}}\big)\le U^{n+1}_i\le
\max\big(\max_iU^n_i,\,c_{\mathrm{bdry}}\big)$: the implicit march is
unconditionally $\ell^\infty$-stable, with no CFL restriction, and cannot
create new extrema.
\end{corollary}

\begin{remark}[What the $M$-matrix argument does and does not prove]
\label{rem:mmscope}
\Cref{prop:mmatrix,cor:dmp} give inverse positivity, order preservation and a
maximum principle for the call \emph{values}. They do \emph{not} by
themselves imply nonnegative second differences: a bounded, monotone call
vector can still be locally concave, so ``the scheme cannot manufacture
negative densities'' would need a separate convexity-preservation proof for
the actual stencil and boundary elimination (a discrete Fokker--Planck
argument), which this note does not carry. The honest statement is the one
production implements: the scheme is monotone and stable, and butterfly and
calendar cleanliness are \emph{measured} on the output grid, per fit
(\cref{sec:diagnostics}); the arbitrage-cleanliness tests in the suite are
regression checks on selected surfaces, not a general proof.
\end{remark}

\begin{proof}
Monotonicity is $B^{-1}\ge0$ applied to $U^n-V^n\ge0$. For the bounds, let
$M$ be the larger of $\max_iU^n_i$ and $c_{\mathrm{bdry}}$: by the unit row
sums $B(M\1)\ge M\1$ componentwise, and $M\1$ dominates the right-hand side
of \eqref{eq:implicit}, so $U^{n+1}\le M\1$ by inverse positivity; the lower
bound is symmetric.
\end{proof}

Strict diagonal dominance is also why no pivoting is ever needed: each step is
a no-pivot Thomas march --- the kernel the compiled march of \cref{app:perf}
vectorizes. (The scalar listing below is the \emph{algorithm}; the production
kernel \texttt{affine\_march.\_march} is its multi-RHS vectorization with the
sensitivity columns as the contiguous SIMD inner loop.)

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={The no-pivot tridiagonal (Thomas) solve for one
implicit step \eqref{eq:implicit} --- the scalar algorithm that
\texttt{models/localvol/affine\_march.py} vectorizes; matches a dense solve to
$10^{-10}$.},label={lst:thomas}]
def thomas(a, b, c, d):        # a: sub-, b: main, c: super-diagonal
    n = len(d); cp = np.empty(n); dp = np.empty(n)
    cp[0], dp[0] = c[0]/b[0], d[0]/b[0]
    for i in range(1, n):      # forward sweep (no pivoting: Prop. M-matrix)
        m = b[i] - a[i]*cp[i-1]
        cp[i], dp[i] = c[i]/m, (d[i] - a[i]*dp[i-1])/m
    x = np.empty(n); x[-1] = dp[-1]
    for i in range(n-2, -1, -1):
        x[i] = dp[i] - cp[i]*x[i+1]
    return x
\end{lstlisting}

\subsection{Why not Crank--Nicolson}
\label{sec:cn}

\begin{remark}[CN loses monotonicity exactly when it matters]
\label{rem:cn}
Crank--Nicolson replaces \eqref{eq:implicit} by
$(I-\tfrac{\Delta T}{2}A)U^{n+1}=(I+\tfrac{\Delta T}{2}A)U^{n}$. The implicit
factor is still an $M$-matrix, but the \emph{explicit} factor has diagonal
$1-\tfrac{\Delta T}{2}\nu_i(a^-_i+a^+_i)$, which is nonnegative only under the
CFL-like bound $\Delta T\le2/\big(\nu_i(a^-_i+a^+_i)\big)$ --- violated
precisely on coarse strike grids with high local variance. When it is
violated the scheme is second-order accurate but \emph{not} monotone, and the
payoff kink excites the classic CN oscillations. This is not hypothetical: the
opt-in Rannacher/CN scheme (two implicit start-up steps damping the kink
\cite{GilesCarter2006}, then CN) bought only $\sim1.1\times$ and produced a
measured coarse-$x$ arbitrage violation on a test surface --- so the default
stays fully implicit, trading one order of time accuracy for
\cref{cor:dmp}.
\end{remark}

\subsection{Observation operator and no-arbitrage diagnostics}
\label{sec:diagnostics}

The observation operator reads the normalized call at each quote's $(\tau,x)$
off the marched solution by interpolation. Cleanliness of the output is
\emph{measured}, with the production tolerances stated exactly: the per-expiry
minimum second difference (butterfly proxy) must be $\ge-10^{-6}$, adjacent
maturities may not decrease by more than $10^{-9}$ (calendar), and prices must
sit in $[-10^{-9},\,1+10^{-9}]$ (bounds) --- all three surfaced as diagnostics
and an \texttt{arbitrageFree} flag. Two further layers: the fit quality is
additionally repriced on a \emph{converged} operator ($\dd\tau/4$, $\dd x/2$;
\texttt{rmsConvergedBp}) because in-operator residuals are blind to
time-discretization error the optimizer compensates; and a separate, finer
log-strike Crank--Nicolson/Rannacher validator
(\texttt{models/localvol/pde.py}; atlas) checks the production march against
an independent discretization.

\section{Variance swaps}
\label{sec:varswap}

A variance-swap quote is the fair strike of realized variance --- for a
diffusion, determined by the expected integrated local variance to expiry. Two
distinct objects share the section: the \emph{total} variance
$I(\tau)=\E\!\int_0^\tau\nu(s,X_s)\dd s$, which is what the residual block
prices, and the quoted \emph{annualized fair strike} $I(\tau)/\tau$. Two
pricers:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \textbf{Static replication} (default): the log-contract weights
\begin{equation}
I(\tau)=2\!\int_0^1\frac{P(\tau,x)}{x^2}\,\dd x+2\!\int_1^{\infty}\frac{C(\tau,x)}{x^2}\,\dd x,
\label{eq:logcontract}
\end{equation}
integrated by trapezoid over the \emph{PDE strike grid} itself --- no separate
replication grid --- with the put leg starting at the \emph{fixed} normalized
strike $x=0.01$ (\texttt{varswap\_k\_lo}; the $x^{-2}$ weight diverges at
zero), not at the lowest quoted strike.
\item \textbf{Source PDE} (opt-in, \texttt{varSwapMethod=source\_pde}): solve
one \emph{backward} PDE
$\partial_tg+\tfrac12\nu x^2\partial_{xx}g+\nu=0$, $g(\tau,\cdot)=0$, and read
$I(\tau)=g(0,1)$ --- the Feynman--Kac representation of
$\E\!\int_0^\tau\nu(t,X_t)\dd t$. It reuses the same tridiagonal machinery
marched backward and carries \emph{analytic} sensitivities
$\partial I/\partial\theta$ (and $\partial I/\partial a$ for the free left
slope) through the same multi-RHS trick. Its selling point is stated
carefully: $g(0,1)$ is determined by the diffusion \emph{around} $x=1$, so it
is materially less sensitive to the far strike boundary than the
$x^{-2}$-weighted replication tail --- but it still runs on the finite
$[0,x_{\max}]$ domain with approximate boundary conditions, so ``no
truncation tail'' would be too absolute. Note~08 develops both in full.
\end{itemize}

\section{What the data identifies, and the objective}
\label{sec:objective}

\subsection{What the data identifies}
\label{sec:identify}

Before writing the objective, be honest about the inverse problem. A typical
fit has $m=n_t\times n_x\approx100$--$200$ nodal parameters (plus the free
left slope $a$ when a var-swap is quoted) against a few dozen to a couple of
hundred vanilla quotes concentrated near the money of a handful of expiries
--- and even where counts balance, vanillas constrain local variance only
through smoothing integrals along diffusion paths. Sparse vanillas therefore
do \emph{not} uniquely identify the grid: whole regions (deep wings, the
sub-front rows, late times beyond the last expiry) are weakly determined,
and what the calibration returns is one \emph{regularized representative} of
an equivalence class. Each regularizer picks a direction of that choice: the
roughness block selects smoothness toward $\theta_{\text{ref}}$, the box
bounds clip the unidentified extremes, the (chained) front tie pins the
sub-front rows to the first identified row, the prior baskets pull toward
yesterday where quotes are silent, and the seed decides which basin a
non-convex solve lands in. Low \emph{quote} error consequently does not mean
the latent surface was recovered --- the synthetic example of
\cref{sec:examples} now measures both numbers separately.

\subsection{The objective}

With unweighted residual blocks $r_\bullet$ (each defined below \emph{without}
its weight), the fit minimizes a stacked, bound-constrained least-squares
residual over the decision vector --- $\theta$ alone on the hot path, or
$(\theta,a)$ with $a\in[0,20]$ when the left slope is fitted (its column
enters the Jacobian through the extrapolation basis):
\begin{equation}
\min_{\substack{\theta\in[\nu_{\mathrm{lo}},\nu_{\mathrm{hi}}]^m\\ (a\in[0,20])}}
\ \tfrac12\Big(
\|r_{\text{opt}}\|^2+\|r_{\text{vs}}\|^2+\|r_{\text{bsk}}\|^2
+\lambda\|L(\theta-\theta_{\text{ref}})\|^2
+w_{\text{cvx}}\|r_{\text{cvx}}\|^2+w_{\text{ft}}\|r_{\text{ft}}\|^2\Big),
\label{eq:lv_objective}
\end{equation}
i.e.\ each weight appears \emph{once}: production stacks the rows
$\sqrt{w_\bullet}\,r_\bullet$, whose squared norm reproduces
\eqref{eq:lv_objective} exactly. The blocks:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em]
\item \textbf{Option residuals} $r_{\text{opt},q}=(P_q(\theta)-y_q)/\eta_q$ in
vega-normalized forward-call units, with
$\eta_q=0.01\max(\text{vega},10^{-3})/\sqrt{\omega_q}$; or the
bid--ask/haircut band hinge with a small mid anchor of weight $0.05$
(\texttt{midAnchorWeight}) when band edges are present (Note~07).
\item \textbf{Var-swap residuals} $r_{\text{vs}}=(I(\theta)-z)/\zeta$ in total
variance (Note~08). One scalar per quoted expiry: it constrains the
\emph{global tail integral} \eqref{eq:logcontract}, biasing the deep-put mass
in aggregate --- it does not uniquely identify the deep-put tail's shape.
\item \textbf{Operator baskets} $r_{\text{bsk}}$: the prior-persistence
operators (Note~13) enter as \emph{signed baskets} of option prices --- one
residual per ATM/RR/BF operator, preserving the risk-reversal and butterfly
coupling rather than scattering it into synthetic quotes.
\item \textbf{Roughness} $L(\theta-\theta_{\text{ref}})$ at weight $\lambda$:
$L$ is the \emph{spacing-aware} second difference (cell-width-normalized
divided differences, reducing to $(1,-2,1)$ on a uniform grid, exact
curvature on a non-uniform one), with time-vs-strike balance
\texttt{gridRegRho}. This is the smoothness that lets a coarse grid
generalize.
\item \textbf{Convex wing} (opt-in) at weight $w_{\text{cvx}}$: a one-sided
hinge $\relu(-D^2\sigma)$ on the nodal local \emph{vols}, confined to
vertices at or below the $5\Delta$ put \emph{and strictly below the deepest
observed quote} --- the extrapolation tail only (\cref{sec:cases}).
\item \textbf{Front tie} (on by default) at weight $w_{\text{ft}}$: the
one-sided differences $\theta_{i,:}-\theta_{i+1,:}$ tying the unidentified
sub-front rows to the first data-identified expiry row --- the single $t=0$
row normally, the full chain at effective weight $\ge1$ on short fronts
(\cref{sec:griddesign}).
\end{itemize}

\section{Sensitivities: tangent and adjoint}
\label{sec:sens}

Everything the optimizer needs follows from differentiating the stepped system
\eqref{eq:implicit}. Since $\nu_\theta$ is \emph{linear} in $\theta$
\eqref{eq:p1}, so is $A(\theta)$, with
$\partial A/\partial\theta_\ell=A[\phi_\ell]$ --- the same stencil
\eqref{eq:stencil} with $\nu$ replaced by the basis function $\phi_\ell$.

\begin{proposition}[Discrete tangent = same factor, extra right-hand sides]
\label{prop:tangent}
The sensitivity $s_\ell^n=\partial U^n/\partial\theta_\ell$ obeys
\begin{equation}
B^{n+1}s_\ell^{n+1}=s_\ell^{n}+\Delta T\,A[\phi_\ell]\,U^{n+1},
\qquad s_\ell^0=0,
\label{eq:tangent}
\end{equation}
the discretization of the continuous sensitivity PDE
$\partial_Ts_\ell=\tfrac12\nu x^2\partial_{xx}s_\ell+\tfrac12\phi_\ell
x^2\partial_{xx}c$. All $m$ sensitivities share the \emph{same} tridiagonal
factor as the value solve, so the whole Jacobian is one factorization plus $m$
extra back-substitutions per step --- $O(N_TN_x\,m)$, and the reason the
vectorized march carries the columns as its inner SIMD loop.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Differentiate $B^{n+1}(\theta)U^{n+1}(\theta)=U^n(\theta)$ in $\theta_\ell$:
$B^{n+1}s^{n+1}_\ell+(\partial_{\theta_\ell}B^{n+1})U^{n+1}=s^n_\ell$, and
$\partial_{\theta_\ell}B^{n+1}=-\Delta T\,A[\phi_\ell]$ by linearity of
$A$ in $\theta$. (Sensitivities against FD: test-locked at $10^{-6}$.)
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Discrete adjoint = one backward sweep]
\label{prop:adjoint}
Let $J(\theta)=f\big(HU^{1..N}(\theta)\big)$ be any scalar objective reading
the marched solution through observation rows $H$. Define adjoint states by the
backward recursion
\begin{equation}
(B^{n})\T\lambda^{n}=H_n\T\,\partial f_n+\lambda^{n+1},
\qquad \lambda^{N+1}=0,
\label{eq:adjoint}
\end{equation}
where $H_n\T\partial f_n$ collects the objective's direct dependence on step
$n$. Then
\begin{equation}
\frac{\partial J}{\partial\theta_\ell}
=\sum_{n}\lambda^{n}{}\T\,\Delta T\,A[\phi_\ell]\,U^{n},
\label{eq:adjgrad}
\end{equation}
at cost one backward PDE solve, $O(N_TN_x)$, plus at least $O(m)$ output
accumulation over \eqref{eq:adjgrad} --- the PDE cost independent of the
parameter count $m$.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Form the Lagrangian
$\mathcal L=f(HU)-\sum_n\lambda^n{}\T\big(B^nU^n-U^{n-1}\big)$ (boundary terms
suppressed). Stationarity in each $U^n$ gives \eqref{eq:adjoint};
$\partial\mathcal L/\partial\theta_\ell$ then picks up only the explicit
$\theta$-dependence of $B^n$, which is $-\Delta T A[\phi_\ell]$, giving
\eqref{eq:adjgrad}. This is summation-by-parts: the transpose of a lower
block-bidiagonal system is upper block-bidiagonal, i.e.\ a backward march with
the \emph{transposed} tridiagonal factors.
\end{proof}

What production actually runs is narrower than the theory above, and the
distinction matters. Production marches \emph{all} forward sensitivities
\eqref{eq:tangent}, materializes the dense option Jacobian from them, and
evaluates both products as matrix products: \texttt{apply\_jacobian} is $Jv$,
and the transpose product is literally $J\T w$ on the assembled matrix (plus
a sparse regularization block) --- \emph{not} the backward recursion
\eqref{eq:adjoint}. The matrix-free GN
solver's win is therefore purely in the \emph{linear algebra}: it avoids
forming $J\T J$ and the dense per-iteration SVD, but its data Jacobian is
still assembled from forward sensitivities at $O(N_TN_x\,m)$ per evaluation.
The true PDE adjoint of \cref{prop:adjoint} --- which would make gradient
cost independent of $m$ --- is derived here as future theory and is
\emph{not currently implemented}. What is test-locked is the implemented
pair: the product identity $\inner{Jv,w}=\inner{v,J\T w}$ and the gradient
against finite differences.

\section{Solvers and the speed story}
\label{sec:solvers}

The cold fit of \eqref{eq:lv_objective} was, at $\sim533$ vertices, an
$\sim86$-second affair hitting the 200-evaluation cap --- the heaviest single
computation in the app. Driving it down is most of the engineering in this
model, and the lessons are worth stating because several plausible ideas
\emph{failed}.

\subsection{Where the time goes}

On a cold fit the cost splits roughly as: optimizer linear algebra / dense SVD
$\sim52\%$, PDE sensitivity march $\sim32\%$, Jacobian assembly $\sim14\%$,
value solve $\sim2\%$. No single per-evaluation lever moves the total ---
which is why four separate ``faster per evaluation'' attempts (coarse grid,
first Numba try, Rannacher, and the \emph{first} matrix-free GN) each won only
$\sim1.1$--$1.2\times$ and were shelved.

\subsection{What worked}

\begin{perfbox}
The cold fit is now $\sim3$--$6\times$ faster than the banded baseline,
recalibrations near-instant. The levers, each with its honest scope:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item \textbf{Stall-based early-stop.} Track the option-block misfit and stop
at the best iterate once it stalls. Fewer evaluations scale march, assembly
\emph{and} optimizer together: measured $1.45\times$ (SPY, 200$\to$109 evals)
to $3.3\times$ (NVDA, 200$\to$41) --- at a \emph{deliberately accepted}
$+0.10$--$0.25$ vol bp drift from the full-cap fit. Window $0$ is
byte-identical.
\item \textbf{Compiled vectorized-Thomas march.} A \texttt{@njit} no-pivot
Thomas with the sensitivity columns as the contiguous SIMD inner loop and the
source fused into the forward sweep: $6.1$--$6.9\times$ the LAPACK banded
march \emph{march-only}, exact to $\sim10^{-15}$. The first Numba attempt
(column-outer scalar Thomas) won only $1.2\times$ --- the lever was the
\emph{loop order}, not the compiler.
\item \textbf{Matrix-free Gauss--Newton} (default \texttt{lvSolver=gn}),
scoped precisely: it engages only for the smooth \emph{mid} fit with the
Numba kernel active and no free-$a$ var-swap path; band/haircut objectives,
var-swap fits and the no-Numba path all run TRF. Where it engages:
$1.3$--$1.65\times$ over TRF with an automatic TRF fallback on breakdown ---
and the public schema states the accepted trade openly: GN can converge to a
slightly \emph{different} real-data optimum, up to $\sim0.25$ vol bp (often
better). The locking tests compare GN and TRF surfaces with tolerances; they
do not prove an identical optimum.
\item \textbf{Warm seeds.} Previous-surface seed on recalibration ---
$\sim38\times$ \emph{on a small synthetic ALPHA test}, qualitatively large on
real chains --- and a parametric-Dupire cold seed ($1.3$--$1.8\times$).
$\theta_{\text{ref}}$ is held fixed so the regularized \emph{objective} is
unchanged; a non-convex bound-constrained solve from a different start is
still not guaranteed the same solution, and the lock is the objective
identity plus the observed agreement, not a uniqueness theorem.
\end{enumerate}
The order-of-magnitude wins are spent; the remaining levers are incremental.
\end{perfbox}

\begin{caution}[title={Caution (historical, pre-Numba): where GN lost}]
This paragraph records the \emph{first} GN verdict, before the compiled
march existed, because its lesson generalizes even though its conclusion was
superseded by the default-GN promotion above. At that time, removing the
dense SVD made fits \emph{slower} at $\le440$ vertices, because the
bottleneck was the march, not the SVD; the clean synthetic benchmark (zero
residual, interior optimum) hid this --- GN converged in 8 evaluations there
but blew past the cap on stiff real data. Once the Numba march collapsed the
per-evaluation cost, the SVD share dominated and the same GN won. The durable
lessons: judge speed-ups on real chains, distrust a synthetic-only win ---
the same benchmark discipline later caught the convex-wing regression
(\cref{sec:cases}) that a synthetic test could never see.
\end{caution}

\section{Case files}
\label{sec:cases}

\begin{example}[title={Case file: the six-day weekly that broke the strike grid}]
\textbf{Setup.} A true 6-DTE SPY weekly (the Massive capture
\texttt{lv\_weekly\_massive.json}; the Bloomberg fixture's shortest expiry is
three weeks, which is why the bug survived the benchmark). Normal expiries fit
at a few vol bps.

\textbf{Failure.} The weekly fit was catastrophic: $108$ vol bp RMS against
the parametric model's $\sim47$ on the same quotes --- the LV surface visibly
missing the smile's curvature.

\textbf{Diagnosis --- measure first.} A pure side-channel diagnostic
(\texttt{affine\_diag.py}) counts, per expiry: in-range strike vertices
(Cause~A), vega-floored quotes (Cause~B), PDE time steps (Cause~C), active
prior rows (Cause~E). The counts acquitted the usual suspects --- time steps
adequate, prior inactive, vega floor latent but not binding --- and convicted
Cause~A: the delta strike axis is sized to the \emph{longest} expiry and
clipped to the global range, so the weekly's narrow smile landed
\emph{three} vertices on its sharpest curvature (\cref{fig:rescue}).

\textbf{Fix.} Two gated changes. (i)~A per-expiry coverage floor
(\texttt{gridXMinPerExpiry}$=8$): split the widest in-range gaps until every
expiry has $\ge m_{\min}$ vertices inside \emph{its own} traded range ---
densifying only under-covered short fronts. (ii)~A short-expiry-aware PDE
step: refine the shared $\Delta x$ to a fraction of the smallest ATM
$\sigma\sqrt\tau$, snapped so $x=1$ stays a node ($0.3\times$, capped at
$400$ nodes, at the time of this case; since tightened to $0.15\times/800$ by
the daily-ladder pass of \cref{sec:griddesign}).

\textbf{Verdict.} $108\to\sim28$ vol bp from the coverage floor alone (the
short-end noise floor), $23.5$ with the refined step --- now \emph{better}
than the parametric fit --- while every long expiry and every normal surface
is byte-identical (both gates test-locked). This case is the historical seed
of the short-end design, not its current entirety: the July-2026 daily-ladder
stack of \cref{sec:griddesign} (adaptive floor, time refinement, even-gap
coverage, chained tie) extends it.
\end{example}

\begin{figure}[H]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.95\textwidth]{fig_lv_rescue.pdf}
\caption{The short-dated rescue, computed by the production grid builder. The

\begin{example}[title={Case file: the convex wing that flattened SPY}]
\textbf{Setup.} The convex-wing hinge (\cref{sec:objective}) exists to keep
the \emph{unquoted} deep-put extrapolation convex. A user ran with a saved
\texttt{gridXNodes}$=20$ --- denser than the default 12.

\textbf{Failure.} SPY's surface RMS degraded to $25.7$ vol bp (the ``26 bp''
regression) on the Bloomberg benchmark; NVDA looked fine.

\textbf{Diagnosis.} The constraint originally selected \emph{every} vertex at
or below the $5\Delta$ put, regardless of data. At 20 strike nodes several of
those vertices sit \emph{inside} SPY's densely quoted put wing, and the hinge
fought the quotes --- forcing the wrong wing on a low-vol name. NVDA's
naturally convex wing had hidden the bug, and so had the default grid: only
the denser grid pushed constrained vertices into quoted territory.

\textbf{Fix.} Confine \texttt{convex\_cols} to vertices at or below the
$5\Delta$ put \emph{and strictly below the deepest observed quote} --- the
extrapolation tail only.

\textbf{Verdict.} SPY $25.7\to2.6$ vol bp, NVDA unchanged; the confinement is
test-locked. The lesson echoes Notes~05 and~09: \emph{a shape constraint must
not be imposed where the data already speaks} --- the same principle, at the
de-Am input, the MCS output, and here the LV extrapolation tail.
\end{example}

\section{What is original, and limitations}
\label{sec:original}

The $P_1$-positivity guarantee and forward-calibration philosophy are
Andreasen--Huge; the contributions here are depth and discipline. The
\emph{vectorized-Thomas Numba march} (loop order as the lever, $6.5\times$
march-only); the \emph{stall early-stop} that scales the whole fit; the
\emph{matrix-free GN} first rejected, then correctly promoted when the march
made the SVD dominant --- consuming the forward-assembled Jacobian through
the two matrix products of \cref{sec:sens}; the \emph{source-PDE var-swap}
with analytic sensitivities; and the \emph{measure-first} short-dated
diagnosis that convicted the strike grid on counts rather than hunches. Each
was validated against a real benchmark that twice caught regressions a
synthetic test would have missed.

Two clarifications of scope. First, this note's object is the \emph{direct
affine fit} --- the surface calibrated to quotes. The application also
carries a separate \texttt{/localvol} path that \emph{extracts} a
local-variance grid from the displayed parametric slices via the Dupire
formula of \cref{app:ref}; it is not merely a validator --- the exact
sticky-local-vol scenario transport currently prices off that extracted
grid --- but it is a different object with different error characteristics.
Second, limitations: a pure diffusion cannot make a true short-dated event
spike (Note~11's variance clock absorbs scheduled events instead); the
tensor grid wastes vertices in the corners (the future non-tensor bowtie
grid is where the GN no-SVD advantage will finally dominate); the true PDE
adjoint remains unimplemented (\cref{sec:sens}); the extrapolation region
sits outside the positivity guarantee (\cref{sec:p1}); the converged-reprice
gap on short fronts is open (\cref{sec:examples}); and the fit, though much
faster, remains the heaviest computation in the app.

\section{Performance notes}
\label{app:perf}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.6em]
\item \textbf{Numba march} (\texttt{lvFastKernel}). Factor-once no-pivot Thomas
(\cref{prop:mmatrix} is why no pivoting), $m$ sensitivity columns as the
contiguous inner SIMD loop, fused source: $6.1$--$6.9\times$ vs LAPACK banded,
exact to $\sim10^{-15}$; graceful banded fallback without Numba.
\item \textbf{Early stop} (\texttt{lvEarlyStop}). Best-iterate stop on
option-block stall: $1.45\times$ (SPY) to $3.3\times$ (NVDA) at
$+0.10$--$0.25$ vol bp; window $0$ is byte-identical.
\item \textbf{Matrix-free GN} (\texttt{lvSolver=gn}). Preconditioned LSMR on
the tangent/adjoint products of \cref{sec:sens}; no dense SVD.
$1.3$--$1.65\times$ over TRF at tensor sizes; golden-locked to the TRF
optimum; automatic TRF fallback; sparse CSR regularization block.
\item \textbf{Warm starts.} Previous-surface recalibration $\sim38\times$;
parametric-Dupire cold seed $1.3$--$1.8\times$; $\theta_{\text{ref}}$ fixed so
the optimum is unchanged.
\item \textbf{Solver scaling and tolerances.} \samepage
\texttt{x\_scale='jac'} plus termination tolerances relaxed
$10^{-12}\to10^{-8}$ on the TRF path: the fit is governed by quote noise,
vega floors and bands, so the tighter tolerance only bought iterations ---
evaluation count cut, surface identical (the atlas records the resulting
defaults).
\item \textbf{Shelved (documented in the perf roadmap):} coarse-grid calibration
(biases $\theta$ by up to $\sim26$ vol points --- non-viable), first Numba
attempt (wrong loop order, $1.2\times$), Rannacher/CN ($\sim1.1\times$ and a
measured coarse-$x$ arbitrage --- \cref{rem:cn}), GN for the non-smooth band
objective, thread/process parallelism (GIL-negative). Five dead-ends
confirming the cost is distributed, not localized.
\end{enumerate}

\begin{caution}[title={Caution (engineering): the field crash that pretended to be a matrix}]
The one production crash in this machinery was an interface bug, not a math
bug: \texttt{LinearizedJacobian} deliberately has no \texttt{.T} attribute
(it is an operator, not a matrix), and the early-stop handler, reached only
on the rare GN$\to$TRF fallback path that then stalls, called
\texttt{jac.T @ r} to compute the final gradient --- an
\texttt{AttributeError} in the field (the R1 backtest finding). The fix
dispatches to \texttt{apply\_jacobian\_transpose}, and the exact
fallback-then-stall path is now test-locked. Lesson: an object that
\emph{pretends} to be a matrix must either implement the whole contract or
make each unsupported member fail at construction, not mid-fit.
\end{caution}

\section{Reference implementation: Dupire extraction}
\label{app:ref}

Forward calibration never differentiates the data (\cref{sec:dupire}), but the
\emph{inverse} map --- reading a local-variance grid off an already-fitted
total-variance surface $w(k,T)$ --- is Gatheral's formula
\cite{Gatheral2006}, used for grid export, the parametric cold seed of
\cref{sec:solvers}, and the graph LV projection (Note~14). The denominator $g$
is the butterfly function: $g\le0$ marks strike arbitrage in the implied
surface, where no positive local variance can reproduce it --- returned as
\texttt{nan}, never clipped. Matches production to $10^{-13}$.

\begin{lstlisting}[caption={Dupire/Gatheral local variance from an implied
total-variance surface (distilled from \texttt{models/localvol/dupire.py}).}]
def dupire_local_variance(k, w, wk, wkk, wt):
    r = k/w                              # w = w(k,T), with k/kk/T derivs
    g = 1 - r*wk + 0.25*(-0.25 - 1/w + r*r)*wk*wk + 0.5*wkk
    return np.where((w > 0) & (g > 0),   # g <= 0: butterfly arbitrage -> nan
                    wt/np.where(g > 0, g, 1.0), np.nan)
\end{lstlisting}
